About

Sky (SKY) is the governance token of the Sky Protocol, which is the rebranded and restructured evolution of MakerDAO — one of the foundational protocols of decentralized finance. The Sky Protocol is responsible for maintaining USDS, the protocol's primary decentralized stablecoin (an evolution of the earlier DAI), as well as DAI itself, which continues to circulate and be redeemable. The SKY token replaced MKR, MakerDAO's original governance token, through an upgrade process where holders could convert their MKR at a ratio of 1 MKR to 24,000 SKY.

The core function of Sky Protocol is the same as its predecessor's: users can lock up collateral assets — primarily Ethereum, wrapped Bitcoin, and other major crypto assets — in smart contracts called "vaults" (previously known as Collateralized Debt Positions or CDPs) to generate USDS or DAI against them. Because the stablecoins are always backed by collateral exceeding their value, the system maintains its peg without relying on any centralized reserve or issuer.

SKY token holders participate in the governance of the Sky Protocol, voting on parameters such as which collateral assets are accepted, what the liquidation ratios are, what interest rates apply to different vaults, and how the protocol's treasury is allocated. The governance system is designed to be decentralized, with major decisions requiring broad token-holder participation.

The Sky Protocol also features the Sky Savings Rate (SSR), the new name for what was previously the DAI Savings Rate (DSR), which allows holders of USDS to earn a baseline yield by depositing their stablecoins into the savings contract. This mechanism has been a popular DeFi feature for users seeking a simple way to generate yield on dollar-denominated holdings without taking on additional risk beyond the smart contract itself.

History

The story of Sky begins with MakerDAO, one of the earliest and most influential projects in decentralized finance. MakerDAO was founded by Rune Christensen, a Danish entrepreneur, who began developing the concept around 2014 and formally launched the protocol and the DAI stablecoin on the Ethereum mainnet in December 2017. At the time, DAI was introduced as a novel solution to a fundamental problem in crypto: how to create a dollar-pegged asset that did not rely on a centralized issuer holding real dollars in reserve.

In its early form, DAI was backed exclusively by ETH and governed by MKR token holders. Over time, the protocol expanded to accept a wider range of collateral assets, and in 2020, a major upgrade introduced Multi-Collateral DAI (MCD), which allowed assets beyond ETH to be used as collateral. MakerDAO also introduced the DAI Savings Rate, a feature that paid holders a yield on their DAI deposits, making it one of the first DeFi savings products.

Throughout the 2021 DeFi boom, MakerDAO became one of the largest protocols by total value locked (TVL), as billions of dollars worth of collateral flowed in to generate DAI. The protocol navigated several stress events, most notably the March 2020 "Black Thursday" market crash, where a rapid collapse in ETH's price caused a cascade of liquidations and briefly threatened the system's solvency before being stabilized.

Rune Christensen unveiled the "Endgame" plan in 2022 — a sweeping restructuring proposal for MakerDAO designed to make the protocol more resilient, efficient and decentralized over the long term. A core component of Endgame was the creation of "SubDAOs" — semi-independent ecosystem units — and the rebrand to Sky. The rebrand officially took effect in 2024, with MKR becoming convertible to SKY and DAI being upgraded to USDS within the new Sky Protocol framework.

Recent Developments

Since the rebrand took effect, Sky Protocol has continued to operate as one of the leading decentralized stablecoin systems in DeFi, with significant TVL maintained across its vault system. DAI remains one of the most widely circulated decentralized stablecoins in the crypto ecosystem, used across hundreds of DeFi protocols for lending, liquidity provision, payments and yield farming. USDS is positioned as the upgraded, Endgame-era successor.

The SKY token has faced mixed market reception. Some long-standing MakerDAO community members expressed skepticism about the rebrand, preferring the familiar MKR token and questioning whether the Endgame plan represented the right direction for the protocol. Others supported the changes as necessary for the protocol's long-term evolution. The conversion ratio of 1 MKR to 24,000 SKY fundamentally changed the token's market dynamics and price presentation, which added to the adjustment period for the community.

On the technical side, Sky Protocol continues to evolve its collateral framework, governance systems and the SubDAO structure. The protocol has also worked to ensure that DAI and USDS maintain their USD peg even under market stress conditions, through mechanisms such as Peg Stability Modules that allow direct exchange between USDS and other regulated stablecoins.

Sky Protocol's ability to maintain a decentralized, overcollateralized stablecoin at scale remains one of DeFi's foundational demonstrations that dollar-pegged assets do not need to rely on centralized custodians. As the DeFi landscape continues to grow and stablecoin regulation matures globally, Sky Protocol's long track record, large TVL and deep integration across the DeFi ecosystem continue to give it a distinctive position in the market.
