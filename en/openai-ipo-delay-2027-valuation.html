OpenAI Weighs Pushing IPO to 2027 Despite $852B Valuation and $2B Monthly Revenue

OpenAI filed a confidential S-1 with the SEC in May 2026 targeting a valuation between $730 billion and $852 billion. Weeks later, Reuters reported the company is now considering waiting until 2027 — as projected 2026 losses top $14 billion.

Published MAXGAZINE Desk · 2026-06-26
AD SPACEIn-article bannerBook this spot →

OpenAI took a major step toward becoming a public company when it confidentially filed an S-1 registration statement with the US Securities and Exchange Commission on May 22, 2026 — formally beginning the process that could lead to one of the largest technology IPOs in history. But according to a Reuters report from late June, the company is now weighing whether to push the actual listing to 2027, citing market conditions and the complexity of its ongoing corporate restructuring.

The company's internal projections place its 2026 revenue at roughly $24 billion to $25 billion — growing at a pace four times faster than Alphabet and Meta achieved at comparable revenue stages. Revenue has crossed $2 billion per month, with enterprise customers now accounting for more than 40% of that total. Despite this growth, OpenAI remains heavily loss-making, with internal documents projecting losses of approximately $14 billion in 2026 as it continues investing in compute infrastructure, model development, and talent.

Valuation range and underwriters

Reported target valuations cluster between $730 billion and $852 billion, though some analyst projections have floated figures near or above $1 trillion. Goldman Sachs and Morgan Stanley are leading the deal. The company's most recent private funding round, closed in early 2026, implied a valuation of approximately $830 billion.

The choice of underwriters signals serious intent: Goldman and Morgan Stanley together ran some of the largest tech IPOs of the past decade. However, underwriting mandates do not guarantee a listing timeline, and both banks have advised clients on delayed offerings before.

Why consider waiting until 2027?

Several factors complicate an immediate 2026 listing. OpenAI completed a for-profit restructuring in 2025 that converted its unusual capped-profit structure into a more standard public-benefit corporation, but that transition and its associated governance questions have attracted regulatory scrutiny. The company also faces ongoing litigation and questions about its relationship with Microsoft, which holds a significant commercial partnership and has co-invested billions into the company.

US equity markets have also been volatile in mid-2026, with rising rate expectations and a broader technology sector sell-off making IPO windows more uncertain than they appeared at the start of the year.

Competitive context

A 2027 delay would come as rivals accelerate. xAI, Elon Musk's AI company, has been valued at over $100 billion in private markets. Anthropic filed confidentially for an IPO of its own in early 2026 at a reported $965 billion valuation. The AI race has no shortage of capital, but the public markets window is narrow and competitive.
