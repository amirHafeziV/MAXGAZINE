Circle and Nomura Plan a 24/7 Stablecoin Settlement Layer for Japan's $440 Billion Daily FX Market

Circle and Japanese banking giant Nomura have announced a joint platform targeting the settlement of Japan's massive corporate FX market using USDC, with a target launch by 2027. The plan would let Japanese companies convert yen to USDC and clear cross-border payments in minutes rather than days — the first time a licensed stablecoin has been positioned as direct replacement infrastructure for traditional correspondent banking in Japan.

Circle Internet Financial and Nomura Holdings have announced a joint venture targeting Japan's corporate foreign exchange settlement market, with the companies describing plans to allow Japanese businesses to convert yen directly into Circle's USDC stablecoin and clear cross-border transactions in near real-time rather than the two-to-three days typically required through traditional correspondent banking channels.

The partnership, reported June 25, targets a 2027 launch and is aimed at Japan's corporate FX market, which Bank for International Settlements data shows handles approximately $440 billion in daily transactions. The service will focus initially on high-value import, export, and overseas investment flows — areas where settlement delay creates meaningful cost and counterparty risk.

The Regulatory Foundation That Made This Possible

The announcement follows Japan's Financial Services Agency clearing USDC under updated payment rules, making it the first dollar-denominated global stablecoin approved for domestic corporate use in Japan. That regulatory clearance — alongside Japan's broader framework for payment stablecoins established in 2023 — has created conditions that regulators in Europe and the United States have not yet matched for large-scale institutional stablecoin settlement.

For Nomura, whose FX book processes significant yen-dollar and yen-euro volume for Japanese corporations with international operations, the partnership represents both an infrastructure upgrade and a business line extension. By deploying USDC as the settlement layer, Nomura aims to eliminate the correspondent banking intermediaries that add both cost and settlement latency to the present system.

Why This Matters Beyond Japan

The Circle-Nomura deal is one of the clearest examples yet of an established financial institution committing to blockchain-based settlement not as an experiment but as core infrastructure for a major existing business. Previous stablecoin-in-finance announcements have often been pilots, proofs of concept, or limited to synthetic instruments. This arrangement targets Nomura's live FX operation.

If the 2027 launch timeline holds and the platform sees meaningful adoption among Japanese corporates, it will represent the first time a regulated stablecoin has displaced traditional correspondent banking infrastructure at institutional scale for a major G7 economy. That outcome would provide a template — and a competitive pressure — for banks in other major economies to move toward similar models.

The deal also directly benefits Circle as the company approaches its own IPO. USDC adoption at institutional scale in one of the world's largest FX markets provides Circle with a growth narrative that goes significantly beyond retail cryptocurrency usage, positioning the stablecoin as utility infrastructure rather than a speculative asset.
