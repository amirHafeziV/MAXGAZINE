Alphabet Closes $84.75 Billion Equity Raise — The Largest AI Infrastructure Financing in Corporate History

Alphabet's equity raise, announced at $80 billion on June 1 and upsized to $84.75 billion at pricing, closed June 30. The structure includes a $10 billion Berkshire Hathaway private placement — Warren Buffett's largest single technology investment — with 2026 capex guidance of $180 to $190 billion and even higher spending planned for 2027.

Alphabet's equity capital raise, announced on June 1 at $80 billion and upsized to $84.75 billion at pricing on June 2, closed on June 30 as the largest equity financing in corporate history undertaken by a major technology company for AI infrastructure. The proceeds are designated for AI compute capacity, data centers, and global infrastructure.

Structure of the Raise

The $84.75 billion raise is structured across three instruments. A $30 billion underwritten public offering, managed by Goldman Sachs, JPMorgan, and Morgan Stanley, includes Class A and Class C common stock alongside depositary shares representing mandatory convertible preferred stock; the underwritten portion was oversubscribed and upsized at pricing from the initial $25 billion to $30 billion. A $40 billion at-the-market offering program for Class A and Class C shares begins in Q3 2026. And a $10 billion private placement to Berkshire Hathaway, split between $5 billion of Class A shares at $351.81 per share and $5 billion of Class C shares at $348.20, completed at a discount of more than 6% to Alphabet's June 1 closing price.

Berkshire's Strategic Bet

For Berkshire Hathaway, the Alphabet investment represents one of the largest single technology bets in the conglomerate's history, adding to a position Berkshire began building in Q3 2025. The investment thesis rests on Alphabet's fundamentals rather than its recent market trajectory: approximately $174 billion in annual operating cash flow, a $460 billion contracted Cloud backlog, approximately 2 billion monthly consumers reached by Gemini-powered products, and more than 30 data centers across 40 cloud regions. Berkshire entered at a meaningful discount to Alphabet's February 2026 highs, which the talent departure and market-cap wipe of the week of June 18-24 created as an entry opportunity.

The Spending Commitment

Sundar Pichai told investors at the June 2 investor presentation that demand for Alphabet's AI solutions from enterprises and consumers currently exceeds available compute supply. Hardware and engineering improvements have reduced the cost of core AI responses by more than 30% since the launch of Gemini 3. The company's 2026 capital expenditure guidance is $180 to $190 billion — by any measure the largest annual infrastructure spending commitment in Alphabet's history — with Pichai signaling that 2027 capex will increase further. The stated rationale is straightforward: every dollar of AI compute deployed generates more than a dollar of marginal revenue at current utilization rates, and Alphabet can borrow or raise capital at rates that make the investment spread positive. Whether those unit economics hold as competition intensifies and as AI inference costs evolve will determine whether the $84.75 billion raise is remembered as a defining strategic move or an overpayment at the peak of the AI infrastructure cycle.
