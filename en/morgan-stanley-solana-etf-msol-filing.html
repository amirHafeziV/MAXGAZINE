Morgan Stanley Files for Spot Solana ETF as SOL Funds Cross $1 Billion in Assets

Morgan Stanley, the Wall Street giant with $1.5 trillion in advisory assets, filed for a spot Solana trust under the ticker MSOL on June 20, 2026, joining a growing cohort of issuers as cumulative net inflows into existing Solana ETFs crossed $1.1 billion since their May 2025 launch.

Morgan Stanley registered the Morgan Stanley Solana Trust with the SEC on June 20, 2026, using the ticker MSOL. The product is structured as a spot trust that would hold Solana (SOL) directly, mirroring the format of the spot Bitcoin and Ethereum ETFs now standard across Wall Street.

Why the Filing Matters

Morgan Stanley's wealth management platform serves more than 15,000 financial advisors and manages approximately $1.5 trillion in client advisory assets. A Solana product on that platform would expose SOL to a segment of investors who have historically relied on their wealth managers to vet and custody crypto products.

The MSOL filing comes at a specific moment in the Solana ETF market. Spot SOL ETFs launched in the United States in late May 2025 — more than a year after spot Bitcoin ETFs — and have crossed $1.1 billion in cumulative net inflows, with Bitwise (BSOL) and Fidelity (FSOL) leading in assets under management. Morgan Stanley's entry would add significant distribution weight to the Solana ETF ecosystem.

Solana's Market Context

As of late June 2026, Solana was trading near $70–$80, down significantly from its record high of approximately $295 reached in November 2024. SOL has faced selling pressure alongside the broader crypto market and has been among the harder-hit assets in the ongoing spot ETF outflow cycle that began in late April 2026.

Despite the price weakness, Solana's on-chain fundamentals remain notable. The network consistently processes several thousand transactions per second, maintains sub-cent fees for most operations, and hosts the majority of retail-focused DeFi and memecoin activity in the current crypto cycle.

Regulatory Dynamics

The SEC approved spot Solana ETFs in May 2025 under a relatively permissive framework. Applications had been pending since 2023 across multiple issuers including 21Shares, VanEck, Bitwise, and Grayscale. The Trump administration's more accommodative stance on digital assets cleared a pathway that prior SEC leadership had blocked.

Morgan Stanley's own previous entry into crypto was notable: in 2021 and 2023, it was among the first major banks to offer Bitcoin funds to wealthy clients. Its move into Solana in June 2026 extends that pattern and signals that SOL has achieved the threshold of institutional credibility required for wealth management inclusion.

Institutional Outlook for SOL

Analysts at 21Shares and other research firms have noted that while the current ETF market is experiencing outflows across BTC, ETH, SOL, and XRP simultaneously, the long pipeline of institutional applications — of which Morgan Stanley's MSOL is the latest — suggests structural demand building beneath the surface. For MSOL to launch, it would need SEC effectiveness, a process that has typically taken three to six months for new crypto ETF products following initial registration.
