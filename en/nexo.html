About

NEXO is the native utility token of the Nexo platform, a centralized cryptocurrency financial services company that offers crypto-backed loans, interest-earning accounts, and a credit card linked to digital assets. The NEXO token provides holders with a tiered loyalty system that determines the interest rate on loans, the yield on deposited assets, and eligibility for the platform's dividend program.

Token Utility

Nexo distributes a portion of its profits to NEXO token holders through a dividends program. Holders who maintain a certain percentage of their portfolio in NEXO qualify for higher interest rates on deposits, lower borrowing rates on loans, and cashback rewards on the Nexo card. The loyalty tiers run from base to platinum, with the most significant benefits unlocked when NEXO constitutes a meaningful share of the user's total portfolio on the platform.

Business Model

Nexo's core product is the instant crypto credit line: users deposit cryptocurrency as collateral and receive a credit line in fiat or stablecoins without selling their crypto. This model allows holders to access liquidity without triggering a taxable sale, while Nexo earns interest on the borrowed amount.

On the other side of the balance sheet, Nexo accepts deposits from users who want to earn yield on their crypto or fiat holdings. The platform deploys these deposits through lending to institutional and retail borrowers, as well as through other yield-generating activities, and passes a share of the return back to depositors.

The NEXO token itself has a fixed maximum supply and is traded on major exchanges. Its price is influenced by the perceived health of the Nexo business, the size and regularity of dividend distributions, and broader sentiment in the centralized crypto finance sector.

History

Nexo was founded in 2018 by Antoni Trenchev and Kalin Metodiev, both based in Europe, along with a founding team with backgrounds in law, finance, and technology. The company launched with a specific pitch: it would be the first platform to offer instant loans backed by cryptocurrency, allowing holders to access cash without selling their digital assets.

The 2018 ICO and Early Years

Nexo conducted an initial coin offering in May 2018, raising approximately $52.5 million. At the time, the concept of crypto-backed loans was novel, and Nexo positioned itself as a crypto equivalent of a traditional collateralized loan — similar to borrowing against a brokerage account or using a home equity line of credit, but with crypto as the collateral. In its early months, Nexo also introduced a dividend model that would distribute 30% of net profits to NEXO token holders, a feature that became a defining characteristic of the token's value proposition.

Growth Through the 2020–2021 Bull Market

The 2020–2021 crypto bull market was a period of rapid growth for Nexo. As crypto prices rose and the yield-farming trend attracted millions of new users to the space, Nexo's earn product — offering interest on deposited cryptocurrencies — gained significant traction. The platform reported billions in assets under management and expanded its range of supported assets and currencies.

The 2022 Shakeout

The collapse of the Terra/LUNA ecosystem in May 2022, followed by the broader crypto market downturn and the failures of Celsius Network and BlockFi later that year, represented the most significant stress test in Nexo's history. While Celsius and BlockFi froze withdrawals and ultimately filed for bankruptcy, Nexo maintained withdrawals throughout and publicly stated it had no significant exposure to the failed platforms. This distinction gave Nexo a substantial reputational advantage in a sector where trust had been severely damaged.

However, Nexo did not emerge unscathed from 2022. In December 2022, law enforcement agencies in Bulgaria raided Nexo's offices as part of an investigation. Shortly after, Nexo announced it would wind down operations in the United States, citing an "inability to find common ground" with US regulators. In January 2023, Nexo reached a $45 million settlement with US state regulators without admitting wrongdoing.

Recent Developments

Following its 2023 US regulatory settlement, Nexo has worked to rebuild its position in regulated markets. The company has pursued additional licenses in various jurisdictions and has shifted its public messaging toward compliance and institutional credibility. This strategy stands in contrast to the path of its now-defunct competitors Celsius and BlockFi, whose collapses came to define the risks of centralized crypto yield products.

Platform Evolution

Nexo has expanded beyond its original crypto loan product to become a broader digital asset wealth management platform. The Nexo card, which allows spending against crypto holdings without selling them, has attracted users in Europe and other markets. The platform also added trading functionality, letting users swap assets directly within the Nexo interface, and institutional services for professional clients.

Regular dividend distributions to NEXO token holders have been maintained where regulations allow. These distributions represent one of the few examples of a crypto platform paying consistent cash-equivalent returns to its token holders based on actual business revenue — as opposed to inflationary token rewards.

Risks and Considerations

The NEXO token's value is closely tied to the operational health and reputation of the Nexo company. As a centralized platform, Nexo carries counterparty risk that DeFi protocols do not. The 2022 regulatory scrutiny, including the Bulgarian investigation which remained ongoing for some time, introduced uncertainty about the company's long-term operating environment in Europe. How Nexo navigates evolving regulatory frameworks, particularly under Europe's MiCA regime, will be a material factor in the platform's future growth and the token's utility.
