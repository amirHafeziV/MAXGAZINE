CLARITY Act Returns to Senate as Crypto Classification Bill Eyes 2026 Passage

Updated Senate text reignites legislative momentum for landmark digital asset framework; crypto markets signal relief on reduced regulatory uncertainty.

Published MAXGAZINE Desk · 2026-06-09
AD SPACEIn-article bannerBook this spot →

The CLARITY Act—a bill designed to establish clear regulatory definitions and oversight rules for digital assets in US markets—has re-entered Senate focus following the release of updated legislative text in May 2026, signaling renewed political will for passage before year-end.

Prediction markets have lifted odds of passage to historically elevated levels, suggesting legislative consensus may be building across party lines. The bill addresses a critical gap in US regulation: the lack of coherent classification for cryptocurrencies, determining which assets fall under securities law (SEC jurisdiction), commodities regulation (CFTC oversight), or alternative pathways.

What CLARITY Would Do

The updated framework proposes tiered regulation based on asset utility and function. Bitcoin and major cryptocurrencies with primary use as stores of value or payment media would receive commodity classification. Tokens with governance or yield-bearing features would face heightened disclosure requirements similar to securities, though potentially with lighter-touch oversight.

The bill also addresses staking, yield farming, decentralized exchanges, and custody—core DeFi activities that have operated in regulatory limbo. Clarity on these operations would unlock institutional capital currently sidelined by compliance uncertainty.

Market Implications

Institutional investors have signaled they are waiting for regulatory clarity before deploying significant capital in digital assets. A CLARITY Act passage would remove a major overhang, potentially unlocking billions in dormant institutional interest.

Smaller altcoins and DeFi protocols face tougher scrutiny under the updated text, but major stablecoins and established cryptocurrencies benefit from the move toward defined rules rather than enforcement-driven uncertainty. Crypto market sentiment has improved incrementally on the news, though broader macro headwinds continue to dominate short-term price action.
