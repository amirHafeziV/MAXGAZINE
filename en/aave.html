About

Aave is a decentralised, non-custodial liquidity protocol that enables users to earn interest on deposits and borrow assets without relying on a traditional financial intermediary. It operates through smart contracts deployed on Ethereum and several other EVM-compatible blockchains, creating permissionless money markets where lenders supply assets to earn yield and borrowers draw against collateral they have deposited.

The protocol introduced several innovations to the DeFi lending space. Among the most notable is the flash loan — a mechanism that allows users to borrow any amount of liquidity without collateral, as long as the loan is repaid within the same transaction block. Flash loans opened up new strategies for arbitrage, collateral swaps and liquidations, and demonstrated a form of lending with no direct equivalent in traditional finance.

Aave also pioneered variable and stable interest rate options, giving borrowers more predictability on their financing costs when needed. The protocol uses over-collateralised lending — borrowers must deposit more value than they borrow — to manage default risk without requiring identity verification or credit checks.

The AAVE token is the governance and safety module token of the protocol. Holders can stake AAVE in the Safety Module, which acts as a backstop against potential shortfall events, earning staking rewards in return. AAVE holders also participate in governance by voting on protocol proposals, including changes to risk parameters, supported assets, fee structures and protocol upgrades.

History

Aave began life in 2017 as ETHLend, a peer-to-peer lending platform built on Ethereum where borrowers and lenders were matched directly. The project was founded by Stani Kulechov, a Finnish law student, who raised $16.2 million in an initial coin offering in 2017. While ETHLend introduced the concept of collateralised crypto lending, the peer-to-peer matching model made it difficult to source liquidity efficiently.

In 2018, the team rebranded from ETHLend to Aave (the Finnish word for "ghost") and pivoted from peer-to-peer lending to a liquidity pool model. This shift was pivotal: rather than matching individual lenders and borrowers, the new Aave model aggregated liquidity from all depositors into shared pools, enabling instant borrowing at algorithmically set interest rates. The Aave V1 protocol launched on Ethereum mainnet in January 2020.

The protocol launched at an ideal time — the DeFi summer of 2020 saw explosive growth in decentralised protocols, and Aave quickly accumulated billions of dollars in total value locked. The introduction of flash loans in V1 drew significant developer attention and became one of the protocol's most distinctive features. Aave V2 followed in 2020 with improvements to capital efficiency including collateral swapping and credit delegation.

AAVE governance token holders voted to migrate from the original LEND token to AAVE in a 100:1 conversion, implementing the Safety Module architecture that made staked AAVE serve as a last-resort backstop for the protocol. By 2021, Aave had expanded to multiple networks including Polygon, Avalanche and others, further growing its total value locked. Aave V3, released in 2022, introduced cross-chain functionality, supply and borrow caps, and efficiency mode — features designed to improve risk management and capital efficiency across Aave's multi-chain deployment.

Recent Developments

Aave has remained one of the largest DeFi protocols by total value locked across multiple market cycles, a testament to the depth of liquidity its pools have attracted and the trust users have placed in its smart contract infrastructure. The protocol's multi-chain presence across Ethereum, Arbitrum, Optimism, Polygon, Avalanche, Base and other networks means its liquidity base is distributed across a large share of the EVM ecosystem.

In recent years, Aave has worked to expand beyond simple lending into new areas. The Aave DAO approved the development and launch of GHO, a decentralised stablecoin native to the Aave ecosystem. GHO can be minted by users who provide collateral to Aave, creating a new type of protocol-native stable asset and adding a new dimension to the Aave product suite. GHO launched on Ethereum mainnet and has been progressively integrated across the protocol's deployments.

Aave governance — operated through AAVE token holders — has shown a high level of activity, debating and voting on risk parameters, new asset listings, cross-chain deployments and fee structures. The protocol has also published frameworks for real-world asset integration, exploring ways to bring tokenised bonds and other off-chain assets into its lending pools. As DeFi continues to evolve, Aave's combination of liquidity depth, governance activity, multi-chain reach and brand recognition positions it as one of the most enduring and consequential protocols in the decentralised finance ecosystem.
