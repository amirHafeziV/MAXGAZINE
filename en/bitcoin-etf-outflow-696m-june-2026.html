Bitcoin Falls Below $60K as ETF Outflows Hit $696M — The Worst Week Since Launch

The crypto market endured one of its most punishing weeks in recent memory. On June 26, 2026, Bitcoin made its first daily close below $60,000 since the third quarter of 2024. Simultaneously, US-listed spot Bitcoin ETFs recorded $696.3 million in net outflows in a single day — the largest daily withdrawal of June — pushing monthly outflows to $3.61 billion and year-to-date losses to $4.6 billion. This report examines the scale of the crisis, the institutional dynamics driving it, and what may lie ahead.

From October 2025's Historic Peak to a 57% Collapse

US spot Bitcoin ETFs reached their all-time high in October 2025, with total assets under management hitting $169.5 billion. Since then, a roughly 50% decline in Bitcoin's price combined with relentless outflows has shrunk total AUM to approximately $72.6 billion — a 57% drop. WalletPilot data shows these funds collectively hold 1.24 million BTC, with roughly 63,500 BTC leaving over the past 30 days.

BlackRock's IBIT, the world's largest Bitcoin ETF, sits at the epicenter of this rout. The Block reports that the average IBIT investor is now down approximately 40%. This was the second-worst week for Bitcoin ETFs on record, representing a crisis of institutional confidence rather than a routine price correction.

Institutional Selling Pressure: Strategy Pulls Back Too

One of the clearest signals of weakening institutional demand is Strategy's dramatic slowdown. Michael Saylor's firm bought more than 50,000 BTC in April and roughly 25,000 in May, but acquired only about 3,600 BTC in June — a sharp deceleration that has fueled debate about whether the leveraged-treasury model can survive a prolonged bear market. The company even sold 32 BTC in a brief reversal, one of the rarest moves in its accumulation history.

Strategy's STRC perpetual preferred shares, designed to trade at $100, closed at $75.69 on June 26 — 24% below par. Bitcoin options expiry this week added further mechanical selling pressure, while Ripple CEO Brad Garlinghouse publicly said Saylor has hurt the crypto market through his debt-funded buying strategy.

Where Is Bitcoin Headed? Capitulation Risk Looms

On-chain data cited by Cointelegraph shows approximately 50,000 BTC were moved at a loss — a signal analysts associate with capitulation risk. If panicked holders begin selling en masse at losses, a secondary wave of price decline could follow. The market Fear and Greed Index stands at 32, firmly in fear territory.

On the other hand, the Bitcoin Power Law model — which traces BTC's long-run growth curve mathematically — frames a price around $58,000 as statistically normal for this stage of the four-year cycle. However, US tech stocks entering deep bear market territory, with Bitcoin's historically high correlation to the Nasdaq, compounds the downside risk. Key support levels analysts watch sit between $54,000 and $58,000.

Conclusion

The $696 million daily ETF outflow is not a seasonal blip — it reflects a genuine crisis of institutional confidence. When the world's largest Bitcoin fund (IBIT) leaves average investors down 40%, and the market's most prominent institutional buyer (Strategy) pulls back sharply, the warning signal is hard to dismiss. Until Bitcoin holds $58,000 on a sustained basis, capitulation risk and further ETF redemptions remain live threats. For investors, weekly ETF flow data — not the spot price — is the most honest real-time gauge of where sentiment actually stands.
