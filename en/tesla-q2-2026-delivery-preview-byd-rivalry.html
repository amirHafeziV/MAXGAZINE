Tesla's Q2 2026 Delivery Report Is Days Away — What 406,000 Vehicles Would Mean

Wall Street expects Tesla to report roughly 406,000 vehicle deliveries for Q2 2026—a 12% sequential gain that would re-establish momentum after a weak 2025. The results, due July 2, will also test whether Tesla has decisively retaken the global EV sales lead from BYD following its Q1 turnaround.

Tesla is scheduled to release its second-quarter 2026 production and delivery figures on or around July 2, and analyst consensus has coalesced around 406,024 total vehicle deliveries—a figure that would represent a 12% increase from the 358,000 units delivered in the first quarter.

The Q2 consensus also calls for 392,662 Model 3/Y deliveries and roughly 13,000 units of other models, including the Cybertruck, remaining Model S and X, and the Tesla Semi. Energy storage deployments are expected to reach 13.8 GWh, a 36% jump over Q1's 8.8 GWh that would underscore the growing weight of Tesla's non-automotive revenue line.

The BYD Context

The delivery figure matters beyond Tesla's own trajectory because it arrives at an inflection point in the Tesla-BYD rivalry. BYD dethroned Tesla as the world's top fully-electric vehicle seller on an annual basis in 2025, with BYD shipping 2.26 million battery-only cars versus Tesla's 1.64 million as broader demand softened amid the expiry of U.S. federal EV tax credits and political controversy surrounding Elon Musk.

However, Q1 2026 produced a reversal. Tesla captured approximately 13% of the global fully-electric market in the first three months of the year, edging past BYD's 11% as Chinese government subsidy programs expired, temporarily compressing BYD's volumes. Whether that swing in Q1 was a structural shift or a temporary effect of subsidy timing is precisely what Q2 data will help answer.

What Analysts Are Watching

Beyond the headline delivery number, investors will focus on automotive gross margin excluding regulatory credits—a figure that came in at 19.2% in Q1, higher than any quarter in 2025. Sustaining margins above 18% while volumes recover would signal that the Model 3/Y refresh cycle and cost reductions are holding.

Tesla's full earnings call, scheduled for July 22, will provide additional context on the progress of the next-generation affordable model, the status of the Robotaxi rollout, and management's updated volume guidance for the full year. The Q2 delivery preview on July 2 is thus the opening frame of a broader narrative that will play out over the following three weeks.
