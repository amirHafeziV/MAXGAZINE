About

Solana (SOL) is a high-performance blockchain designed to support thousands of transactions per second with very low fees, making it popular for decentralized finance, NFTs, gaming and consumer-facing crypto applications. It achieves its speed through a combination of innovations, most notably a mechanism called Proof of History, which timestamps transactions to help validators process them efficiently in parallel rather than one at a time.

The network runs on a proof-of-stake consensus model, where validators stake SOL to participate in producing and confirming blocks and earn rewards for doing so. Solana's architecture allows a single global state to be updated extremely quickly, which has made it a popular choice for applications that need near-instant finality, such as decentralized exchanges, payment apps and on-chain order books.

SOL itself is used to pay transaction fees (which are typically a fraction of a cent), to participate in staking and help secure the network, and as the base currency for many applications built on Solana. The token has an inflationary supply schedule that started with a relatively higher inflation rate, gradually decreasing over time toward a long-term fixed rate, balancing network security incentives with controlled new-token issuance.

History

Solana was conceived in 2017 by Anatoly Yakovenko, a former Qualcomm engineer, who outlined the Proof of History concept in a white paper as a way to improve blockchain throughput by giving network participants a way to agree on the passage of time. He was joined by Greg Fitzgerald and other co-founders, including Raj Gokal, to build the protocol, with the Solana Foundation overseeing the network's development. The project raised funds through several token sales between 2018 and 2020 before launching its mainnet beta in March 2020.

Solana gained significant attention during the 2021 bull market, when its low fees and high throughput attracted a wave of new decentralized applications, NFT projects and trading platforms, positioning it as a leading alternative to Ethereum for developers seeking cheaper transactions. However, the network also faced a series of notable outages during this period of rapid growth, prompting ongoing engineering work to improve stability and resilience.

A major setback came in late 2022, when FTX — a cryptocurrency exchange closely associated with Solana's ecosystem and an early backer of the project — collapsed, causing SOL's price to fall sharply alongside broader concerns about the network's prospects. Despite this, the developer community continued building, and the network underwent continued technical improvements. By 2023 and into 2024, Solana saw a strong resurgence in activity, driven by renewed interest in decentralized exchanges, meme-token trading and consumer applications, helping it recover much of its standing as one of the most actively used blockchains.

Recent Developments

Solana's ecosystem has continued to grow around decentralized exchanges, on-chain trading platforms and a thriving meme-coin culture that often launches new tokens directly through Solana-based platforms, drawing large volumes of retail trading activity. Beyond trading, the network has attracted interest from payments companies and major consumer brands experimenting with on-chain loyalty programs, ticketing and digital collectibles, leveraging Solana's low costs and fast confirmation times for everyday use cases.

Ongoing technical work continues to focus on further improving network stability, reducing the impact of network congestion during periods of high demand, and refining the validator client software. The approval of Solana-linked investment products in some markets has also opened the door for more traditional investors to gain exposure to SOL, following a pattern similar to Bitcoin and Ethereum. As institutional infrastructure around custody and compliance matures, Solana's combination of speed, low fees and an active developer base continues to support its position as one of the leading platforms for both decentralized finance and consumer-facing crypto applications.
