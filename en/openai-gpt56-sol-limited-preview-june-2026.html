OpenAI Unveils GPT-5.6 Sol, Terra, and Luna in Government-Gated Preview

OpenAI's new GPT-5.6 family introduces three tiered models—Sol for frontier reasoning, Terra for everyday work, and Luna for speed—currently accessible only to roughly 20 government-vetted organizations, with general availability expected around mid-July.

OpenAI began a limited preview of its GPT-5.6 model family on June 26, 2026, introducing three distinct models optimized for different cost and capability trade-offs. The launch continues the company's practice of tiered releases, with general availability expected within two to three weeks.

The flagship model, Sol, is designed for frontier reasoning, long-horizon agentic workflows, and demanding scientific tasks. Pricing is set at $5 per million input tokens and $30 per million output tokens. Terra, the mid-tier offering, delivers performance comparable to GPT-5.5 at half the cost—$2.50 input and $15 output per million tokens—targeting everyday enterprise use cases. Luna, the entry-level model, is priced at $1 input and $6 output, emphasizing speed and affordability for high-volume applications.

Government-Gated Access

In an unusual distribution move, OpenAI has limited initial access to approximately 20 companies whose participation was approved by the U.S. government. Axios reported the arrangement, noting that it reflects a broader pattern in which frontier AI capabilities are being managed under national security considerations before general commercial release.

OpenAI benchmarks show Sol reaching top scores on Terminal-Bench 2.1, the leading coding evaluation, and competitive results on ExploitBench for cybersecurity—using roughly one-third fewer output tokens than comparable systems. Sol also scored 53.5% on the Virology Capabilities Test and 68.4% on Human Pathogen Capabilities in SecureBio evaluations, figures that likely contributed to the government approval requirement before public release.

The GPT-5.6 family represents OpenAI's third major model-series launch in 2026, following GPT-5 in early spring and GPT-5.5 in May. The company's new ChatGPT Business interface is simultaneously introducing a simplified model picker that lets enterprise users select between Sol's deeper reasoning and Luna's faster throughput within the same subscription tier. General availability for all three models is expected by mid-July 2026.
