About

Ethena is a decentralized finance protocol built on Ethereum that issues USDe, a synthetic dollar designed to maintain a $1.00 peg without relying on traditional bank reserves. Where fiat-backed stablecoins like USDC hold dollar deposits in regulated accounts, USDe achieves its stability through a delta-neutral derivatives strategy: the protocol holds long positions in spot cryptocurrencies — primarily Ethereum and Bitcoin — and simultaneously maintains equivalent short positions in perpetual futures on centralized exchanges. The two positions offset each other's price exposure, keeping the net dollar value of the collateral stable.

The ENA Governance Token

ENA is the governance token of the Ethena protocol. Holders can participate in protocol governance votes and are eligible to stake ENA in exchange for protocol rewards. The token's value is linked to the growth of the USDe supply and the usage of the Ethena protocol more broadly.

How USDe Generates Yield

Perpetual futures markets typically require traders who hold short positions to pay a funding rate to traders who hold long positions when the market is in a state of contango — that is, when futures prices trade above spot prices. This condition is common when there is strong bullish sentiment and high demand for leveraged long exposure.

Ethena captures this funding rate: because the protocol holds the long spot position and the short futures position simultaneously, it collects the funding payments from leveraged long traders on the exchange side. These payments are pooled and distributed to holders of sUSDe, the staked version of USDe. During strong bull markets, this yield has been substantial, at times reaching well above 20% annually.

The key risk is the funding rate environment. During bear markets or periods of low demand for leverage, funding rates can turn negative — meaning the protocol must pay rather than receive funding. Ethena maintains a reserve fund to absorb periods of negative funding, but prolonged negative funding could compress or eliminate yield for sUSDe stakers.

History

Ethena was founded by Guy Young, who conceived the protocol while working in traditional finance and observing the structure of carry trades — strategies that profit from the difference in yield between two related instruments. Young recognized that the perpetual futures funding rate in crypto markets was structurally similar to a carry trade, and that it could be captured in a decentralized manner without relying on fiat bank accounts.

Development and Testnet

The Ethena team began building the protocol through 2023, conducting testing and refining the mechanism for managing the delta-neutral position across multiple centralized exchanges. A key design challenge was credit risk: if the protocol's short positions were all on a single exchange and that exchange failed — as FTX did in late 2022 — the collateral would be at risk. Ethena addressed this by distributing positions across multiple exchanges and using off-exchange custody solutions to hold the underlying spot assets separately from the exchange.

Mainnet Launch and Rapid Growth in 2024

Ethena launched on Ethereum mainnet in February 2024. The timing was fortuitous: the crypto market was in an early bull phase, ETH was appreciating, and funding rates on perpetual futures were strongly positive. USDe supply grew from zero to over $2 billion within months of launch, making it one of the fastest-growing stablecoins in DeFi history.

In April 2024, Ethena launched the ENA token through an airdrop to early users and protocol participants. The airdrop rewarded users who had minted USDe, staked sUSDe, or provided liquidity in USDe pools during the protocol's early months. The ENA token listing attracted significant market attention and trading volume.

By mid-2024, USDe had grown to become one of the largest synthetic or algorithmic stablecoins by market cap, and sUSDe was widely integrated into major DeFi protocols as a yield-bearing collateral asset.

Recent Developments

Ethena's ecosystem has expanded significantly since its 2024 launch. USDe and sUSDe are now integrated as collateral assets in a wide range of DeFi protocols, including major lending platforms and decentralized exchanges. The integration of sUSDe — which accrues the protocol's yield directly — into money markets has made it a popular choice for users seeking a dollar-denominated yield that is uncorrelated to traditional interest rate environments.

Expansion to Other Networks

Ethena has extended USDe beyond Ethereum to other blockchain networks, broadening the stablecoin's availability to DeFi users on different chains. The protocol has also explored new collateral types, adding Bitcoin and other assets alongside ETH as backing for USDe minting, which reduces concentration risk in any single asset.

USDtb and Institutional Products

Ethena launched USDtb, a separately structured stablecoin backed by traditional short-term US Treasury assets through a BlackRock fund structure. USDtb provides a low-risk backing option for USDe during periods when the funding rate environment is negative, acting as a stabilizing reserve that the protocol can deploy to reduce risk to the sUSDe yield.

Risk Outlook

The core risk for Ethena remains the funding rate environment. During extended bear markets, perpetual futures funding rates can stay negative for prolonged periods, compressing sUSDe yields and potentially drawing down the protocol's reserve fund. The degree to which USDe can maintain its peg and sUSDe can sustain positive yield across a full market cycle — including a prolonged downturn — remains an open question that the protocol's relative youth has not yet fully answered.
