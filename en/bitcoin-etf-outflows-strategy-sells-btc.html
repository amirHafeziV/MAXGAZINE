Bitcoin Plunges as Record ETF Outflows and Strategy's First BTC Sale Rock Markets

Bitcoin fell below $67K after $4.4B in 13-day ETF outflows and Strategy's surprise BTC sale triggered panic in crypto markets.

Published 2026-06-14 · MAXGAZINE Desk

A Historic Selloff Unfolds

Bitcoin tumbled below $67,000 on June 3, 2026, as US spot Bitcoin ETFs recorded capital outflows for the 13th consecutive trading day — a streak that has now drained more than $4.4 billion from the market since May 15. The selloff represents one of the sharpest institutional exits in Bitcoin's history, outpacing even the turbulent withdrawals seen during the 2022 bear market cycle.

The BlackRock iShares Bitcoin Trust bore the heaviest losses, with approximately $3.3 billion withdrawn over the same 13-day window — roughly 75% of total outflows across all spot Bitcoin ETFs. Other major ETF issuers including Fidelity and Ark Invest also reported net negative flows as institutional confidence in Bitcoin's near-term price trajectory deteriorated sharply.

Strategy's Surprise Sale Amplifies the Panic

Adding fuel to an already anxious market, Strategy — the NASDAQ-listed firm once known as MicroStrategy and long considered one of Bitcoin's most committed corporate holders — sold 32 BTC at an average price of approximately $77,135. The transaction generated around $2.5 million, a relatively small sum for a company holding tens of thousands of coins. Yet the symbolic weight of the sale was enormous: Strategy had not sold a single Bitcoin since 2020, and its shift to selling rattled investor sentiment at a moment when the market could least afford it.

The move triggered a cascade of forced liquidations across leveraged positions. Analysts at Blockhead noted that Bitcoin's intraday low of $65,710 on June 3 represented a decline of over 6% within a 24-hour window, amplified by automated stop-loss orders triggering in rapid succession across major derivatives exchanges.

Fear Grips the Market

The Crypto Fear and Greed Index plummeted to 12 on June 3, deep into "Extreme Fear" territory, a level not seen since the collapse of FTX in November 2022. Since May 15, Bitcoin has lost approximately 21% of its value, falling from $80,000 to around $63,400. The broader altcoin market has suffered even more severe drawdowns, with total cryptocurrency market capitalization falling 48% from its 2026 peak. The macro backdrop has compounded the pain: the ongoing US-Iran conflict continues to delay Federal Reserve rate cuts, keeping risk appetite suppressed across asset classes.
