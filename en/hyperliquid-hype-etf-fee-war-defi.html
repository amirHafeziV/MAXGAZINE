Hyperliquid's HYPE Defies the Crypto Crash — And Now There's a Three-Way ETF Fee War

While Bitcoin tumbled below $65K on June 3, Hyperliquid's HYPE token hit a new all-time high of $75.52. Hours later, Grayscale launched a competing Hyperliquid staking ETF on Nasdaq — igniting a fee war with 21Shares and Bitwise that is reshaping how institutions access DeFi-native assets.

Published 2026-06-03 · MAXGAZINE Desk

On June 3, 2026, while the broader crypto market was deep in a liquidation event that pushed Bitcoin below $65,000, one asset printed a new all-time high: Hyperliquid's HYPE token reached $75.52, marking a roughly 160% gain year-to-date and a fully diluted market valuation approaching $69 billion — larger than the market cap of Nasdaq Inc. itself.

The decoupling was stark enough to draw mainstream financial coverage. HYPE had been rallying independently of broader market trends for months, driven by the success of the Hyperliquid protocol — a decentralized perpetuals exchange that has captured significant trading volume from centralized competitors including Binance and Bybit.

Grayscale Enters the Ring

The same day HYPE hit its all-time high, Grayscale officially launched the Grayscale Hyperliquid Staking ETF (ticker: HYPG) on the Nasdaq Stock Market. The product holds HYPE tokens and stakes them through the Hyperliquid network, targeting the protocol's approximately 2.2–2.3% annual staking yield on top of price exposure.

Grayscale priced HYPG at a 0.29% sponsor fee — a deliberate undercut of two earlier competitors. 21Shares' THYP charges 0.30%, and Bitwise's BHYP launched with a 0% introductory rate that reverts to 0.34% after the first month. The three-way fee competition is a sign of how quickly institutions have moved to package DeFi-native exposure into familiar ETF wrappers.

Combined, HYPE spot ETFs had attracted $139.5 million in net inflows as of mid-June, with no net outflow days recorded across any of the three products.

What Is Hyperliquid?

Hyperliquid is a Layer-1 blockchain purpose-built for on-chain derivatives trading. Unlike most DeFi protocols that run on Ethereum or Solana, Hyperliquid operates on its own high-performance chain with a custom order book — making it fast enough to compete directly with centralized exchanges on execution speed.

HYPE is the native token of the network, used for governance, staking, and fee discounts. The protocol has grown rapidly in 2025–2026, with monthly perpetuals volume at times exceeding that of established rivals.

Why This Matters for DeFi

The HYPE ETF launches represent the first wave of exchange-traded products wrapping a DeFi-native asset that is not Bitcoin or Ethereum. If inflows continue, they signal that institutional demand is evolving beyond the original crypto blue-chips toward protocols with genuine on-chain revenue and tokenomics.

As of mid-June 2026, HYPE's year-to-date gain of approximately 160% contrasts sharply with Bitcoin's decline from its 2025 highs, making it one of the few major tokens with a positive YTD performance during what has been a difficult year for crypto broadly.
