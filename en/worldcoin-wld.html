About

Worldcoin (WLD) is a cryptocurrency and digital identity network built on a single radical premise: in a world increasingly populated by AI-generated content and autonomous bots, proving you are a unique human being will become one of the most valuable capabilities a person can have. The project was co-founded by Sam Altman — CEO of OpenAI — and Alex Blania, with hardware and infrastructure developed by Tools for Humanity, a company the two established to bring the Worldcoin vision to life.

World ID and the Orb

At the core of the project is World ID, a privacy-preserving proof-of-personhood credential. To obtain one, a user visits an Orb — a custom-built, silver spherical device roughly the size of a bowling ball — which scans the iris of both eyes using near-infrared cameras. The Orb converts the scan into a numerical hash called an IrisCode. The raw image is deleted on-device; only the hash is stored, making it computationally impossible to reconstruct the original scan. If the IrisCode is unique in the database, the user receives a World ID: a zero-knowledge proof credential they can present to applications without revealing any personal information beyond the fact that they are a distinct human.

WLD Token and World Chain

The WLD token serves two roles. First, it is distributed as a grant to verified World ID holders, representing the project's commitment to a form of universal economic participation. Second, WLD functions as the governance token of the World network, allowing holders to vote on protocol parameters and treasury decisions. The token launched publicly in July 2023.

The network relies on World Chain, an Ethereum-compatible layer-2 blockchain launched in 2024, built on the OP Stack and part of the Optimism Superchain. World Chain prioritizes transactions from verified humans and gives human-verified users a daily allocation of gas-free transactions, directly linking on-chain utility to biometric identity.

Worldcoin sits at the intersection of decentralized identity, Web3 infrastructure, and AI-safety tooling. Supporters argue that a globally interoperable, privacy-first human identity layer addresses an urgent problem that neither governments nor social media platforms have solved. Critics raise concerns about the biometric data collection model, its concentration of sensitive data, and the ethical dimensions of offering token incentives for iris scans in lower-income markets.

History

Worldcoin traces its origins to conversations between Sam Altman and Alex Blania in 2019. Altman, already leading OpenAI, was thinking about what digital life would look like as AI systems became indistinguishable from humans online. The two began building Tools for Humanity, a San Francisco-based company funded by Andreessen Horowitz, Khosla Ventures, and Reid Hoffman, among others.

Orb Development and Beta Testing (2021–2023)

The project entered a long stealth development phase focused on engineering the Orb — a hardware challenge of considerable difficulty. The device had to scan irises rapidly across all skin tones, lighting conditions, and eye colors, generate a hash on-device, delete the raw biometric, and communicate results securely to a backend. Hundreds of engineering iterations preceded the public launch.

A beta phase ran from 2021 to 2023, during which Orbs were deployed in dozens of cities across Africa, Asia, and Latin America. Users were offered WLD tokens in exchange for iris scanning. This approach was immediately controversial — critics noted that the program disproportionately targeted economically vulnerable populations in the Global South, raising concerns about exploitation and informed consent.

Public Launch and Regulatory Backlash (2023)

The official token generation event occurred in July 2023. The WLD token began trading on major exchanges, and over two million individuals had been verified globally. Regulatory responses came swiftly. Kenya suspended Worldcoin operations in August 2023 over data protection concerns. Germany's Bavarian data protection authority opened a formal investigation. France, Spain, and other EU regulators scrutinized the program under GDPR provisions governing biometric data.

Rebrand and World Chain (2024)

In October 2024, the project rebranded from Worldcoin to simply World, with the WLD ticker retained. The rebrand coincided with the launch of World Chain on mainnet — an Ethereum-compatible layer-2 blockchain built on the OP Stack, designed to prioritize verified human transactions and offer gas-free daily allocations to World ID holders. A dedicated mini-app ecosystem was introduced within the World App, transforming it from a token wallet into a platform for human-verified applications.

US Expansion and Enterprise Partnerships (2025–2026)

In May 2025, World launched in the United States for the first time, opening Orb locations in San Francisco, Los Angeles, Miami, Atlanta, Austin, and Nashville. The US launch coincided with a wave of partnership announcements — Tinder, Zoom, and DocuSign integrated World ID to distinguish human users from AI-driven accounts. In November 2025, Thai authorities ordered the deletion of 1.2 million iris scans and suspended operations, continuing a pattern of regulatory friction that has followed the project across multiple jurisdictions.

Recent Developments

By mid-2026, World has established itself as the largest proof-of-personhood network in existence, with verified humans numbering in the tens of millions across more than 80 countries. The core product proposition has evolved from a cryptocurrency project into a cross-platform identity layer that technology companies are actively integrating into login flows, age verification screens, and anti-bot measures.

Enterprise Adoption

World ID's integrations with Zoom and Tinder were among the most visible milestones of 2025 and early 2026, enabling platforms to differentiate human participants from AI bots and synthetic profiles. DocuSign's integration extended the use case to digital signature authentication, marking an early incursion into regulated identity markets. The project has announced discussions with financial services and healthcare platforms, though the depth of those integrations remains to be confirmed.

WLD Token Dynamics

The WLD token continues to serve as both a universal basic income vehicle for verified users and a governance mechanism for the World protocol. Grants are distributed on a rolling basis to World ID holders, with the per-user amount declining over time according to a pre-set schedule. Governance votes have addressed treasury allocation and grant size adjustments.

Ongoing Debate

Debate about Worldcoin's model remains active. Privacy advocates argue that any system creating a global biometric database — even a hashed one — centralizes sensitive biological data in ways that become targets for state actors and hackers. The project maintains that zero-knowledge proofs and on-device deletion prevent re-identification, and that the alternative — a fragmented, easily gamed, platform-siloed identity landscape — poses equal or greater risks. Regulatory outcomes across Asia, Europe, and the Americas will largely determine whether World can achieve the scale its stated mission requires.
