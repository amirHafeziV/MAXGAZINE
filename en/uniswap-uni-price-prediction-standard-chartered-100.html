Uniswap UNI Price Prediction: Standard Chartered Eyes $100

Standard Chartered set a $100 target for UNI by 2030. We break down the year-by-year path, the tokenization thesis and the risks behind the call.

Published MAXGAZINE SEO Desk · 2026-06-23
AD SPACEIn-article bannerBook this spot →

Uniswap's UNI token has become one of the most-watched names in crypto this week after Standard Chartered initiated coverage with an eye-catching $100 price target. This Uniswap price prediction, which implies a roughly 40x gain from current levels near $2.70, sent the token surging more than 25% in a single session even as bitcoin stalled in a correction. Below we break down the bank's year-by-year path to 2030, the tokenization thesis behind it, and the risks that could derail the call.

Standard Chartered's roadmap to 2030

Geoffrey Kendrick, global head of digital assets research at Standard Chartered, initiated coverage of Uniswap with a $100 target by the end of 2030 — a roughly 40x move from a current price near $2.70. The bank lays out a stair-step path: $6.50 by the end of 2026, $20 in 2027, $40 in 2028, $65 in 2029 and finally $100 in 2030.

That 2026 forecast alone sets the floor for the bank's bullish case and shows the optimism is not confined to the distant future. The market responded quickly, with UNI jumping more than 25% after the report — its most bullish institutional call yet for a DeFi token.

Why Uniswap? The tokenization thesis

The logic behind the optimism is the explosive growth of tokenized assets on-chain. Kendrick expects their value to climb from about $340 billion today to $4 trillion by the end of 2028, with the DeFi share rising from 3.5% to 30% by 2030 — close to a 37x increase in assets active in DeFi. As the world's largest on-chain trading venue, with cumulative volume past $3.4 trillion since launch, Uniswap is positioned to capture much of that flow.

The UNI governance token could also shift from a pure voting tool to an income-generating asset if a fee-sharing mechanism is switched on — a key driver in the bank's valuation. Whale activity in UNI hit a seven-month high after the report, often a sign of accumulation by large investors.

Will UNI reach $100?

Whether UNI reaches $100 hinges on several variables. First, the tokenization thesis requires traditional finance to move on-chain at scale — a trend that has begun but faces regulatory hurdles and fierce competition from rising protocols such as Hyperliquid. Second, activating the "fee switch" that passes value directly to token holders still needs governance approval and is far from certain.

Technically, the recent 25%-plus rally broke a short-term downtrend, but holding above resistance is essential for the move to continue. Investors should treat long-dated bank targets as optimistic scenarios, not guarantees, and remember that the gap between an analyst report and an actual price can take years to close.

Conclusion

Standard Chartered's UNI price prediction is, more than a number, an institutional endorsement of the long-term convergence of traditional finance and DeFi. If real-world asset tokenization plays out as expected, Uniswap stands to be one of its biggest winners. Even so, the gap between $2.70 today and $100 in 2030 is a five-year, highly volatile road tied to dozens of regulatory and technical variables. Investors would be wise to view the target as an optimistic outlook rather than a promise, and to keep risk management front and center.
