Sergey Brin's Memo and Six Lost Researchers: Inside Google's Emergency AI Coding Pivot

Google DeepMind has lost six senior researchers to rivals in five months, triggering a co-founder intervention: Sergey Brin wrote an internal memo calling for urgently closing the gap in agentic AI coding, while the company restructured its coding strike team to include midtraining and raised $84.75 billion to fund the response.

By June 28, 2026, Google DeepMind had confirmed the loss of six senior researchers to competitors within a five-month window: Denny Zhou to Meta's Superintelligence Lab in February; Noam Shazeer — co-designer of the Transformer architecture — to OpenAI on June 18; AlphaFold lead and Nobel laureate John Jumper to Anthropic on June 20; and Jonas Adler, Alexander Pritzel, and one additional unnamed researcher to Anthropic between June 20 and June 25. Together these six departures covered the foundational layers of a frontier AI lab: reasoning research (Zhou), training architecture (Shazeer), scientific AI and biological computing (Jumper), AI coding products (Adler), and pretraining (Pritzel).

Sergey Brin's Internal Memo

Reporting from The Information, published June 25, confirmed that Sergey Brin — Google's co-founder, who has become increasingly hands-on at the company since late 2024 — wrote an internal memo directing that Google must 'urgently bridge the gap in agentic execution and turn our models into primary developers of final code.' The language is a direct response to competitive pressure from Anthropic. Google CFO Anat Ashkenazi acknowledged at a recent investor event that Anthropic codes close to 100% of its own work using AI, while Google currently sits at approximately 50%. Brin is defining closing that internal adoption gap as the company's strategic imperative, not merely a product improvement.

The Coding Strike Team Pivot

Google formed its AI Coding Strike Team in April 2026 as an emergency response to Anthropic's dominance in developer tools, initially led by Sebastian Borgeaud and overseen by both Brin and DeepMind CTO Koray Kavukcuoglu. As of late June, the strike team has expanded its mandate to include midtraining — the phase of model development between initial broad pretraining and instruction tuning that allows engineers to expose a model to specialized data to improve specific capabilities. The coding strike team's midtraining work reportedly involves training on Google's proprietary codebase, which would complicate direct public release of any resulting improvements.

Market Consequences

The talent departures triggered an Alphabet market-cap wipe of approximately $269 billion in the week of June 18-24, as analysts revised their estimates of Gemini's competitive trajectory. The selloff created the entry point for Berkshire Hathaway's $10 billion private placement in Alphabet on June 2 — a bet, in Berkshire's framing, that the talent loss does not change Alphabet's fundamental earnings trajectory. Meanwhile, Alphabet's $84.75 billion equity raise — the largest AI infrastructure financing in corporate history — closed June 30, with proceeds designated for compute, data centers, and global AI capacity. The 2026 capital expenditure guidance is $180 to $190 billion, with 2027 expected to increase further. On benchmarks, Gemini 2.5 Pro with Deep Think launched June 22 with the strongest publicly published science and reasoning scores yet recorded (82.4% GPQA Diamond, 94.1% HumanEval Plus). The competitive assessment for the back half of 2026 will turn on whether the coding strike team's midtraining intervention, combined with the capital infusion, is sufficient to close the agentic execution gap Brin identified.
