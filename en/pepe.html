About

Pepe (PEPE) is a meme coin launched in April 2023 on the Ethereum blockchain. It takes its name and imagery from Pepe the Frog, an internet meme character that originated in a 2005 comic by artist Matt Furie and became one of the most widely recognised symbols in internet culture over the following two decades. The token was launched anonymously, has no official team or roadmap, and its whitepaper explicitly describes it as a token with no intrinsic value and no promise of returns — a common framing for meme coins that positions the project as entertainment rather than investment.

Despite its self-described lack of utility, PEPE attracted massive speculative interest shortly after launch, riding a wave of retail enthusiasm that drove its market cap to several billion dollars within weeks. It became one of the fastest-growing assets by market cap in the history of the cryptocurrency market, demonstrating the power of internet culture and community-driven speculation in the crypto space.

As an ERC-20 token on Ethereum, PEPE can be held in any compatible wallet and traded on both centralised exchanges and decentralised platforms. Its total supply is fixed, with the vast majority allocated to the public from the outset and a small portion reserved for centralised exchange listings and future development. There is no staking, no governance mechanism, and no underlying protocol — the token's primary value proposition rests on brand recognition, community size and the speculative demand that meme culture continues to generate.

History

The Pepe the Frog character was created by artist Matt Furie in 2005 as part of a webcomic called "Boy's Club". The character spread virally through early internet communities, particularly 4chan and Reddit, and became one of the defining memes of internet culture through the 2010s. While Furie has long sought to reclaim the character from some of its more controversial uses across the internet, the frog remains one of the most recognisable memes globally.

The PEPE token was launched in April 2023 by anonymous developers who chose the character as the basis for a new meme coin in the spirit of earlier projects like Dogecoin and Shiba Inu. The launch attracted virtually no attention in its earliest days — no presale, no venture capital, no major marketing spend. But within weeks, organic interest spread rapidly across crypto Twitter and other communities, pushing PEPE to viral status and a market cap that briefly exceeded several billion dollars.

The token's rapid rise drew coverage from mainstream financial media and prompted a wave of listings on major centralised exchanges, accelerating its reach to a broader audience of retail traders. This listing cycle — where growing community interest prompts exchange listings which in turn attract new buyers — became a model studied by subsequent meme coin launches.

By late 2023 and into 2024, PEPE consolidated as one of the most actively traded meme coins, with significant volumes maintained even during quieter periods in the broader market. The Pepe brand's cultural familiarity continued to underpin its community, making it one of the few meme coins that retained a substantial and active holder base beyond its initial launch mania.

Recent Developments

PEPE has maintained a position among the top meme coins by market capitalisation, with sustained community activity across social media platforms and continued trading volumes on major exchanges. The coin's cultural roots in internet meme history give it a degree of brand stickiness that many short-lived meme projects have lacked.

The broader meme coin market has grown significantly since PEPE's emergence, with hundreds of new tokens launching across multiple blockchains. However, PEPE has managed to hold its position at the top tier, benefiting from first-mover advantage among Pepe-branded tokens and the scale of community that formed during its initial breakout. That community continues to drive content creation, social engagement and advocacy that sustain attention to the token.

As with all meme coins, PEPE's market performance is driven primarily by sentiment, social momentum and speculative activity rather than protocol fundamentals. Its price has historically shown large swings tied to broader crypto market cycles and surges in retail interest. For observers and participants, PEPE represents the clearest modern case study in how internet culture, viral marketing and speculative capital flows can combine to create durable, if volatile, market presence entirely outside traditional financial frameworks.
