About

OKB is the native utility and governance token of OKX, one of the world's largest cryptocurrency exchanges by trading volume. Originally launched as a means for traders to receive fee discounts and access platform services, OKB has evolved into a multi-purpose asset tied to OKX's broader ecosystem — including X Layer, a layer-2 blockchain built using Polygon's Chain Development Kit (CDK).

Exchange Utility

On the OKX exchange, OKB provides its holders with trading fee discounts, access to token launch events on OKX Jumpstart (the exchange's launchpad), and eligibility for various platform promotions. The more OKB a user holds, the higher the tier of benefits they qualify for, creating a loyalty incentive that parallels the models used by other major exchanges for their native tokens.

Fixed Supply of 21 Million

A defining event in OKB's history was the August 2025 tokenomics overhaul, which fixed the total supply permanently at 21 million tokens — mirroring Bitcoin's hard cap as a deliberate design signal. A one-time burn of over 65 million OKB was executed to reach this supply. The fixed-supply model eliminated the ongoing uncertainty around future issuance and positioned OKB as a deflationary asset whose scarcity is structurally guaranteed.

X Layer: OKB as a Gas Token

X Layer is OKX's layer-2 blockchain built on Polygon's CDK, launched in 2024. It is an Ethereum-compatible network that uses OKB as its native gas token — meaning every transaction on X Layer requires OKB to pay for computation, creating a direct link between network usage and token demand. X Layer is designed to support DeFi applications, NFT markets, and OKX-ecosystem projects, extending OKX's on-chain presence beyond the centralized exchange.

Governance

OKB holders participate in governance decisions related to the OKX ecosystem, including X Layer parameter changes and treasury-related decisions. The governance scope has expanded alongside the platform's on-chain ambitions, giving OKB a role beyond pure exchange utility.

History

OKB's history is inseparable from the history of OKX, the exchange that issued it. OKX was founded in 2017 by Star Xu, who had previously co-founded OKCoin, one of China's earliest bitcoin exchanges. OKX initially operated as OKEx and was incorporated in the Seychelles, offering futures and spot trading for a wide range of cryptocurrencies.

Launch of OKB (2018)

OKB was launched in 2018 as a utility token for the OKEx platform, initially managed by the OK Blockchain Foundation. The token was designed to give traders fee discounts and access to Jumpstart token sales. At launch, 300 million OKB were minted in total, with tokens distributed to users over time through promotions, team allocations, and foundation reserves. The exchange committed to a quarterly buyback-and-burn mechanism, using a portion of trading fee revenues to repurchase OKB from the open market and permanently destroy them.

Rebranding to OKX (2022)

In January 2022, the exchange rebranded from OKEx to OKX, reflecting a broader ambition beyond being perceived as a pure crypto exchange. The rebrand coincided with an expanded product suite including OKX Wallet, OKX NFT Marketplace, and the OKX Web3 developer ecosystem. The OKB token retained its name and ticker throughout the rebrand.

X Layer Launch (2024)

In 2024, OKX launched X Layer — a layer-2 blockchain built using Polygon's Chain Development Kit. X Layer uses OKB as its native gas token, expanding OKB's utility beyond exchange discounts to a transactional on-chain role. The network processes transactions at high throughput and near-zero fees, and is designed to host DeFi applications and OKX-ecosystem projects.

Tokenomics Overhaul and Massive Burn (August 2025)

The most transformative event in OKB's history occurred in August 2025. OKX announced and executed a one-time burn of 65,256,712 OKB — reducing the total supply permanently to 21 million tokens. The number 21 million was chosen deliberately to mirror Bitcoin's supply cap, framing OKB as a scarce, institutionally predictable asset. The announcement triggered a price surge of over 170% within days. OKX also announced that OKB would serve as the exclusive gas token for X Layer going forward, and introduced on-chain governance features for OKB holders in the OKX ecosystem.

Recent Developments

By mid-2026, OKB has stabilized at a significantly higher valuation than before the August 2025 tokenomics overhaul, with the 21 million fixed supply serving as the structural foundation for its market positioning. The token occupies a well-defined role in the OKX ecosystem as the gas currency of X Layer and the primary loyalty asset of the exchange.

X Layer Growth

X Layer has attracted a growing number of DeFi protocols and NFT projects seeking access to OKX's user base and liquidity. As network activity on X Layer grows, OKB demand for gas payments increases, creating a feedback loop between ecosystem adoption and token utility. OKX has actively onboarded projects to X Layer through a dedicated ecosystem fund, investing in developers who build applications on the network.

Competitive Context

OKB competes directly with BNB (Binance's exchange token) and other centralized exchange tokens as stores of value, fee discount vehicles, and on-chain infrastructure assets. The fixed supply of 21 million gives OKB a structural scarcity argument that distinguishes it from exchange tokens with ongoing inflation or unpredictable burn schedules. However, BNB's larger user base, higher trading volume, and more developed DeFi ecosystem remain significant competitive advantages.

Outlook

OKB's value proposition in 2026 rests on three pillars: the fee utility on OKX exchange (one of the world's largest spot and derivatives trading platforms), the gas token role on X Layer, and the fixed-supply scarcity narrative. The token's trajectory depends on OKX's continued market position, X Layer's adoption rate, and the broader demand for on-chain activity in the OKX ecosystem.
