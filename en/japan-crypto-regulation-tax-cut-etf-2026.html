Japan Slashes Crypto Tax to 20% and Opens Door to Bitcoin ETFs in Historic Bill

Japan's lower house passed a sweeping bill on June 11 reclassifying crypto assets as financial instruments, cutting the maximum tax rate from 55% to a flat 20% and paving the way for domestic crypto ETFs.

Japan's lower house of parliament passed landmark cryptocurrency legislation on June 11, 2026, in what analysts describe as the most significant overhaul of the country's digital-asset framework since it first regulated crypto exchanges nearly a decade ago. The bill reclassifies cryptocurrency holdings — including Bitcoin, Ethereum, and XRP — as financial instruments under the Financial Instruments and Exchange Act, shifting oversight away from the less stringent Payment Services Act.

The Tax Overhaul

The most consequential change for individual investors is a dramatic tax cut. Under Japan's current rules, crypto gains can be taxed as miscellaneous income at marginal rates as high as 55% — among the highest rates for crypto investors anywhere in the world. The new legislation sets a flat capital-gains rate of 20%, the same applied to stocks and bonds. The change is scheduled to take effect for the 2028 tax year.

For context, Japan has one of the world's largest retail crypto user bases, and the high tax burden has long been cited by local exchanges as a major obstacle to market growth. The announcement follows years of industry lobbying and aligns Japan's treatment of crypto with international norms already in place in South Korea, Germany, and several other major economies.

Crypto ETFs and Institutional Access

By reclassifying crypto as a financial instrument, the bill also creates a legal pathway for domestic crypto exchange-traded funds — something Japan has not permitted under the Payment Services Act framework. The Financial Services Agency is expected to issue detailed guidelines on ETF structures after the bill passes the upper house, which must still approve it before it can be enacted.

Implementation of the broader regulatory framework is scheduled for 2027. Until then, current rules remain in effect.

New Compliance Requirements

The bill is not solely about tax relief. It introduces stricter rules for new token listings, including investment caps for retail investors and increased penalties for unlicensed operations. Unaudited token offerings face new disclosure requirements, and trading platforms must meet higher capital and cybersecurity standards. The legislation is designed to attract institutional capital while reducing the risk of fraud and market manipulation.

Global Context

Japan's move comes as competing financial centres intensify efforts to attract digital-asset businesses. The United States has advanced the CLARITY Act to provide a federal regulatory framework for crypto, while the European Union's MiCA regulation is now in force. Japan has historically been cautious but thorough in its crypto regulation, and this bill reflects a strategic pivot toward using digital-asset policy as a tool to accelerate capital market growth.
