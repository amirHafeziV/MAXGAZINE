About

Bitget Token (BGB) is the native utility token of Bitget, one of the world's largest centralized cryptocurrency exchanges. BGB serves multiple functions within the Bitget ecosystem: holders receive discounts on trading fees, can participate in exclusive token sales on Bitget Launchpad, and gain access to enhanced features on the platform. The token is also closely tied to Bitget's copy trading product, one of the exchange's flagship offerings, where experienced traders share their strategies with followers.

BGB operates on multiple blockchains and has a fixed total supply with a built-in deflationary mechanism. Bitget periodically uses a portion of its profits to buy back and burn BGB tokens, reducing the circulating supply over time and creating downward pressure on supply. This buy-and-burn model is common among major exchange tokens and aligns the token's performance with the growth of the exchange itself.

Beyond trading discounts and launchpad access, BGB is used for staking within the Bitget ecosystem, where holders can earn additional rewards. The token also functions as collateral for certain features and as a means of participation in governance consultations. As Bitget has expanded its product suite — including spot trading, futures, copy trading and on-chain wallet services — BGB has been integrated across these offerings, giving holders a stake in the exchange's commercial success.

History

Bitget was founded in 2018 and initially focused on cryptocurrency derivatives trading before expanding into spot trading and a broader range of financial products. The exchange grew rapidly, particularly in Asian markets, and launched BGB as its native platform token to deepen user engagement and reward loyalty within the ecosystem.

BGB was introduced to give active users a stake in the platform's growth. Early use cases centred on trading fee rebates and access to token launch events, which helped drive initial adoption. The token gained broader visibility as Bitget scaled its global operations, expanding its user base across Southeast Asia, Europe and Latin America throughout the early 2020s.

A significant milestone for BGB came as Bitget's copy trading feature grew in popularity. By allowing retail investors to automatically replicate the trades of experienced investors, Bitget differentiated itself in a competitive exchange market, and BGB became embedded in this product as a preferred settlement and incentive currency. The exchange periodically reported large buy-back volumes as evidence of its commitment to the token's deflationary model.

By the mid-2020s, Bitget had grown into one of the top cryptocurrency exchanges globally by trading volume, and BGB's market capitalization climbed accordingly. The exchange also expanded into Web3 with its own wallet product, integrating BGB into on-chain activities. Throughout this period, Bitget continued its burn programmes and published transparency reports to build credibility with its growing user base, positioning BGB as both a utility token and a barometer of the exchange's commercial health.

Recent Developments

Today, BGB continues to serve as a central pillar of the Bitget trading ecosystem, with utility embedded across fee structures, staking rewards, launchpad participation and the exchange's copy trading product. Bitget's ongoing expansion into new regions and product verticals — including its Bitget Wallet for on-chain activities — has kept BGB integrated across an increasingly diverse set of use cases.

The exchange's periodic token burns remain a key narrative driver for BGB, with Bitget regularly publishing records of tokens destroyed from circulation. This creates a supply side dynamic that many holders follow closely as a signal of the exchange's financial performance and commitment to the token's long-term value proposition.

Bitget has also moved to improve regulatory standing in several jurisdictions, obtaining licences in key markets as part of a broader push toward compliance. Greater regulatory clarity tends to benefit exchange tokens like BGB by reducing operational uncertainty and potentially opening the door to institutional participation on the platform. As one of the largest global exchanges, Bitget's continued growth in derivatives and spot volumes directly supports the demand side of BGB's economy, making the token's fortunes closely tied to the exchange's competitive positioning in a market dominated by a handful of major players.
