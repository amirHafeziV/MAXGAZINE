About

Pi Network (PI) is a cryptocurrency project designed from the outset to be accessible to ordinary smartphone users without the need for specialized mining hardware or significant energy consumption. Unlike Bitcoin's proof-of-work mining, which requires powerful computers competing to solve cryptographic puzzles, Pi Network allows users to participate in securing the network by simply opening an app on their phone and pressing a button once per day — a process the project calls "mobile mining."

The network relies on an adapted form of the Stellar Consensus Protocol (SCP) for consensus, which uses a system of trusted circles — groups of users who vouch for each other's trustworthiness — to validate transactions without the energy-intensive computation required by traditional proof-of-work blockchains. This design choice makes Pi significantly more lightweight than first-generation cryptocurrencies in terms of energy use.

The PI token serves as the native currency of the Pi Network ecosystem. It is used to pay for goods and services within the Pi marketplace, to tip other users, to pay for access to Pi-native applications, and eventually to participate in the broader DeFi ecosystem being built on the Pi blockchain. The supply of PI is tied to the total number of active users who have been mining within the network, with daily mining rates decreasing over time as the user base grows.

A key component of Pi Network's model is its emphasis on identity verification: users must pass a KYC (Know Your Customer) check in order to migrate their mined PI tokens from the testnet to the open mainnet. This requirement was intended to ensure that each account represents a real, unique human being — distinguishing Pi from projects that could be gamed by bots or duplicate accounts.

History

Pi Network was created by Nicolas Kokkalis and Chengdiao Fan, both of whom hold PhDs from Stanford University, along with Vince McPhilip. Kokkalis specialized in distributed systems and human-computer interaction, while Fan focused on social computing. The project was launched on March 14, 2019 — a date chosen deliberately for its connection to the mathematical constant Pi (π ≈ 3.14) — and quickly attracted significant attention.

During the early phases from 2019 onward, Pi Network operated entirely as a mobile application where users registered and tapped a lightning bolt button daily to accumulate PI tokens. The project referred to this as "mining," though technically the process was more akin to a points-earning loyalty program than actual cryptocurrency mining, as no tokens moved on any blockchain during this phase. The network was structured using a referral system that rewarded early adopters for inviting friends, which drove rapid user growth.

The network went through multiple phases: Phase 1 (design and distribution via app), Phase 2 (testnet launched in 2020), and Phase 3 (enclosed mainnet in December 2021, then open mainnet). During the enclosed mainnet period, users could complete KYC verification and transfer their mined PI to the real blockchain, but transfers to external wallets or exchanges were restricted.

The long-awaited open mainnet launched in February 2025, allowing PI to be traded on external cryptocurrency exchanges for the first time. The launch triggered significant initial trading volume and price discovery as years of accumulated PI began moving onto the open market. The listing on major exchanges marked a pivotal moment for the project, transitioning it from a community-building exercise into a tradable digital asset with real market pricing.

Recent Developments

Since its open mainnet launch in early 2025, Pi Network has entered a new chapter focused on ecosystem development and establishing PI as a currency with real utility. The network's emphasis on a large verified user base — tens of millions of KYC-passed accounts — positions it differently from most cryptocurrency projects, which often start small and grow gradually. Pi's model inverted this, accumulating a vast user community first and then opening the blockchain to trading.

The central challenge for Pi Network in this phase is converting its user base into an active economic ecosystem. The Pi team has promoted the development of Pi-native applications — ranging from marketplaces and games to social platforms — that accept PI as payment, aiming to create real demand for the token beyond speculative trading. Progress in this area will be key to the long-term sustainability of PI's value.

Criticism of the project has included concerns about the centralized nature of the core team's control during the pre-mainnet years, the pace at which KYC processing took place, and skepticism about whether the project's enormous claimed user numbers reflected genuinely active participants. Pi's response has been to continue building its utility ecosystem and to point to its verified user count as evidence of genuine demand.

As Pi Network navigates post-mainnet life, the project's ability to deliver on its promise of a widely used, accessible cryptocurrency will be tested by market forces. The size of the community and the global reach of the project — with particularly strong adoption in Asia, Africa and Latin America — remain distinguishing features that set Pi Network apart from most other cryptocurrency projects of similar vintage.
