Real-World Assets Cross $32 Billion On-Chain as BlackRock and Franklin Templeton Lead TradFi Migration

Tokenized real-world assets excluding stablecoins surpassed $32 billion on-chain in May 2026 — a 200%-plus surge in one year — as BlackRock's BUIDL and Franklin Templeton's BENJI reshape how institutions hold Treasuries, gold, and private credit in the digital age.

Published 2026-06-10 · MAXGAZINE Desk

In a development that would have seemed speculative just three years ago, the market for tokenized real-world assets (RWA) has crossed $32 billion in active on-chain value as of May 2026, according to aggregated data from multiple DeFi analytics platforms. Excluding stablecoins — themselves the earliest and largest form of tokenized RWA — the growth represents more than a 200% increase over the past twelve months.

The numbers mark a structural shift rather than a speculative spike. The institutions now operating in this space include BlackRock, JPMorgan, Franklin Templeton, Ondo Finance, and Hashnote — names that signal genuine capital market adoption rather than crypto-native experimentation.

The Treasury Market: From $380M to $14B

The fastest-growing and best-documented segment is tokenized U.S. Treasuries. What was a $380 million niche in Q1 2023 has grown to approximately $14 billion by Q1 2026 — nearly a 37x increase in three years. BlackRock's BUIDL fund, which issues tokenized shares of a short-duration Treasury fund on Ethereum, crossed $2.4 billion in assets under management and filed two new tokenized fund structures with the SEC in May 2026.

Franklin Templeton's BENJI now operates across eight blockchain networks — the widest institutional coverage of any tokenized fund — offering T-bill exposure on Ethereum, Stellar, Polygon, and five additional chains. Together, BUIDL, BENJI, Ondo Finance's OUSG/USDY, and Hashnote's USYC account for roughly 80% of the tokenized Treasury market.

Beyond Treasuries: Gold and Private Credit

Tokenized gold emerged as a breakout category in early 2026. During the Iran conflict in the first quarter, combined daily trading volumes for PAXG (Paxos Gold) and XAUT (Tether Gold) exceeded $1 billion as investors sought around-the-clock safe-haven access that traditional gold markets could not provide. This real-world demand event validated the use case of tokenized commodities in ways that prior bullish projections had not.

Private credit tokenization is earlier-stage but growing rapidly. Platforms including Centrifuge and Maple Finance have enabled institutional lenders to originate and fractionalize private credit pools on-chain, unlocking liquidity in an asset class traditionally locked behind long hold periods and high minimums.

Why It Matters — and What's Still Missing

The $32 billion on-chain RWA figure, while significant, remains small relative to the tens of trillions held in the underlying traditional asset classes. Analysts broadly agree that the barriers are no longer primarily technological — the infrastructure exists — but regulatory and operational: varying jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction rules on digital securities, fragmented liquidity pools, and the absence of universal secondary market standards.

For the four audiences reading MAXGAZINE across English, Persian, Arabic, and Turkish markets, the relevance is direct: tokenized Treasuries are increasingly accessible to non-US investors via blockchain rails, removing minimums and settlement delays that have historically made US government bonds impractical for retail participation in emerging and frontier markets.

The tokenization trend is also converging with the broader DeFi infrastructure maturation story. As on-chain RWAs grow, they increasingly serve as collateral in DeFi lending protocols — creating a bridge between traditional finance and decentralized finance that, at scale, could fundamentally alter how global capital markets operate.
