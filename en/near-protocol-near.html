About

NEAR Protocol (NEAR) is a layer-1, proof-of-stake blockchain built to make decentralized applications fast, inexpensive and simple enough for mainstream users. Its guiding idea is that ordinary people — and increasingly the AI agents acting on their behalf — should be able to use on-chain services without wrestling with the complexity that defines most crypto networks. NEAR pursues this through human-readable account names, low and predictable fees, and a developer experience designed around familiar tools.

Sharding and consensus

At the heart of NEAR is a sharding design called Nightshade. Instead of forcing every transaction through a single chain, Nightshade splits the network's workload across parallel "shards" that process activity at the same time. This lets total capacity grow as shards are added, rather than congesting as usage rises. NEAR pairs sharding with proof-of-stake consensus, where validators lock up NEAR tokens to take turns producing and verifying blocks, and a mechanism called Doomslug helps the network reach fast, practical finality. More recent upgrades introduced stateless validation, which lowers the hardware required to run a validator and widens participation. Block times have been pushed toward the sub-second range with finality measured in roughly a second.

The NEAR token

The NEAR token powers the network. It is used to pay transaction fees — a portion of which are burned — and to reward validators and the delegators who stake with them. Staking NEAR helps secure the chain and grants a voice in governance decisions. NEAR is also used to pay for on-chain storage, tying the cost of keeping data on the network to its native asset. The protocol issues new tokens to fund staking rewards, while fee burning offsets part of that issuance, making net inflation sensitive to how heavily the network is used.

Account model and usability

A defining feature is NEAR's account system. Rather than long, cryptic strings, users can register readable names such as alice.near, and accounts can hold multiple keys with different permissions. This underpins account abstraction, letting apps sponsor fees on a user's behalf, set spending limits, or enable smoother sign-in flows that feel closer to ordinary web apps. Combined with cheap transactions and fast blocks, the design aims to remove much of the friction that keeps non-technical users away from blockchains.

Use cases and the AI direction

NEAR hosts the usual range of Web3 applications — decentralized finance, tokens, marketplaces and games — but the project has increasingly framed its mission around artificial intelligence. Its founders argue that AI agents will become major users of blockchains, needing a secure, low-cost layer to transact, hold value and coordinate. Work on "intents," where a user states a desired outcome and the network works out how to fulfil it, and "chain abstraction," which lets a single NEAR account operate across many blockchains, is aimed squarely at that future. Taken together, NEAR positions itself as practical infrastructure for both people and the software agents that increasingly act for them.

History

NEAR began not as a blockchain project but as an artificial-intelligence company. In 2017, Illia Polosukhin — a co-author of the landmark 2017 research paper "Attention Is All You Need," which introduced the transformer architecture behind today's large language models — teamed up with Alexander Skidanov, an engineer who had built distributed database systems at MemSQL. Their initial venture, NEAR.ai, explored using machine learning to write code. That work led them toward the problem of paying and coordinating a global pool of contributors, which in turn drew them into blockchain and the challenge of building a network that could scale to millions of users.

Founding and mainnet

The pair founded NEAR Protocol in 2018, raising funding from prominent venture investors and assembling a team to design a sharded, proof-of-stake chain from the ground up. Development was overseen by the NEAR Foundation, a Switzerland-based nonprofit set up to support the ecosystem. The network launched in phases: a restricted MainNet went live in April 2020 with the foundation operating it, control passed to the community of validators in September 2020, and a community vote enabled free token transfers in October 2020 — the point at which NEAR became a fully open, usable network.

Early growth and the Rainbow Bridge

In its first years, NEAR focused on developer tooling, its readable account model and onboarding flows meant to feel familiar to web users. The Rainbow Bridge connected NEAR to Ethereum, allowing assets to move between the two ecosystems, and the Aurora project added an Ethereum-compatible (EVM) environment so Solidity developers could deploy on NEAR. During the 2021 market boom the ecosystem expanded quickly, supported by grants and developer programs, though it also weathered the broad downturn that followed and the wider industry turbulence of 2022.

Scaling milestones

NEAR's defining technical thread has been the rollout of Nightshade sharding in stages. "Simple Nightshade" introduced sharded state, later phases added more shards and, crucially, stateless validation — a redesign that let validators verify blocks without storing the entire state, lowering hardware requirements. A significant 2025 upgrade pushed block production toward sub-second speeds with finality close to a second, improving the responsiveness that consumer and AI applications depend on.

The pivot to AI

From 2024 onward, NEAR's leadership reframed the project around artificial intelligence, arguing that AI agents would become primary users of blockchains and that the network should serve as their settlement and coordination layer. Polosukhin, drawing on his transformer background, presented AI research at industry events and the project launched initiatives spanning confidential computing, agent frameworks, intents and chain abstraction. This shift connected NEAR's origins as an AI company with its present identity, positioning it at the intersection of two of the most closely watched technology trends.

Recent Developments

Today NEAR sits at the meeting point of two narratives: a scalable, low-cost layer-1 blockchain and an emerging platform for decentralized artificial intelligence. Its leadership has leaned heavily into the AI thesis, framing NEAR as infrastructure where autonomous agents can transact, store value and coordinate on behalf of users.

AI infrastructure

NEAR has rolled out a stack of AI-oriented products, including offerings that use confidential computing — running models inside Trusted Execution Environments so that data and prompts stay private — and tools for building and running AI agents. The project's emphasis on "user-owned AI" argues that individuals, rather than large platforms, should control their data and the agents that act for them. These efforts aim to differentiate NEAR from general-purpose chains by giving AI developers reasons to build specifically on NEAR.

Chain abstraction and intents

A second major theme is making blockchains disappear into the background. Through "chain abstraction" and "intents," NEAR is working to let a single account operate across many networks, with users expressing what they want rather than executing each technical step. Chain signatures, which let NEAR accounts control assets on other blockchains, are central to this vision and position NEAR as a coordination hub rather than an isolated island.

Ecosystem and market position

NEAR continues to support decentralized finance, consumer apps, gaming and tokenized projects, backed by the NEAR Foundation's grants and developer programs. As a top-ranked layer-1 by market capitalization, it competes with other smart-contract platforms for developers and users, and its token tends to move with the broader market during macro events. Like many networks, its long-term value will depend on real adoption — whether the AI and chain-abstraction bets translate into sustained usage.

Outlook

NEAR's roadmap points toward faster sharding, larger multi-party computation networks for chain signatures, and deeper AI integration. Whether the project's combination of consumer-friendly design and an AI-first strategy lets it stand out in a crowded field remains an open question, but few networks are as directly tied to the convergence of crypto and artificial intelligence — a theme that keeps NEAR in active discussion among developers and investors.
