Ethereum Hits 2026 Low; SharpLink Buys 5,000 ETH Despite $1.8B Paper Loss

Ethereum has crashed to its 2026 low near $1,512—70% below its 2025 peak. SharpLink Gaming resumed buying after an 8-month pause. Is this the bottom?

Ethereum is trading near its 2026 low of $1,512, down more than 70% from its August 2025 peak of $4,953. Against this backdrop, SharpLink Gaming—a gaming and sports betting firm—resumed Ethereum purchases for the first time in eight months, buying 5,000 ETH at $1,537 on June 26. The question is whether this institutional conviction signals that Ethereum's 2026 bottom is already in.

Why Ethereum Fell to 2026 Lows

Several forces converged to drive ETH's decline. Spot Ethereum ETFs, which many analysts expected to mirror Bitcoin ETF success, instead saw consistent capital outflows in recent weeks. Leveraged position liquidations wiped out over $1 billion in long ETH contracts. On the macro side, a stronger-than-expected U.S. jobs report in early June killed expectations for a Federal Reserve rate cut. When cheap money disappears, risk assets like Ethereum take the first hit.

Geopolitical tensions and five consecutive sessions of Nasdaq losses further weighed on risk sentiment. Ethereum briefly touched below $1,500—a critical psychological floor. A decisive break below this level opens the door to $1,400, analysts say.

SharpLink Gaming's Digital Asset Treasury Strategy

SharpLink now holds 876,285 ETH, making it the second-largest public Ethereum treasury after the Ethereum Foundation itself. At current prices, that's roughly $1.3 billion in assets. The catch: their average acquisition cost is $3,609 per ETH, meaning the company carries an unrealized loss of approximately $1.79 billion.

Rather than reducing exposure, SharpLink raised $75 million through a registered direct offering on June 22–23, specifically to buy more ETH. Then, on June 26, it resumed purchases after an eight-month pause, acquiring 5,000 ETH through FalconX. This playbook mirrors Michael Saylor's Strategy: accumulate through downturns using equity financing, then hold for the cycle recovery. Strategy itself now holds 847,363 BTC, with Saylor teasing additional purchases despite Bitcoin trading below $60,000.

The $1,500 Battle: Where Does Ethereum Go From Here?

The $1,500 level will define Ethereum's near-term direction. A hold here targets $1,750 resistance; clearing that opens $1,800–$2,000. A break below pulls $1,400 into play. The market is split: ETF flows remain negative while corporate accumulators like SharpLink are building positions at 2026 lows.

History suggests that when retail exits and institutions accumulate, inflection points are near. The critical variable is macro: if the Fed pivots or risk appetite returns, Ethereum's 70% discount to its all-time high could attract significant catch-up demand.

Conclusion

Ethereum has hit its 2026 price floor—but defending $1,500 is what matters now. A sustained recovery requires ETF outflows to reverse and broader risk appetite to return. Whether SharpLink's $1.79 billion paper loss turns into a historic win depends entirely on that. If past crypto cycles are any guide, this bet is not yet lost.
