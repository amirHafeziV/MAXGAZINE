BMW's New i3 on Neue Klasse Platform Offers 440-Mile Range and 400 kW Charging

BMW has revealed the second Neue Klasse electric vehicle, the i3 sedan: 440 miles of estimated range, 400 kW ultra-fast charging, 463 horsepower, vehicle-to-load support, and native NACS ports — with European deliveries starting this fall and US deliveries in early 2027.

Published MAXGAZINE Desk · 2026-03-18
AD SPACEIn-article bannerBook this spot →

BMW revealed the second vehicle in its Neue Klasse electric vehicle family on March 18, 2026: the new i3 sedan, which arrives to challenge the Tesla Model 3 and other premium battery-electric competitors with a package of specifications that set new benchmarks in the segment.

The car claims an EPA-estimated range of approximately 440 miles — a figure BMW achieved through a combination of better aerodynamics and the new Neue Klasse battery architecture, which delivers meaningfully higher energy density than the cells used in BMW's current EV lineup. That range claim would make it one of the longest-range non-truck EVs on the market when it goes on sale.

400 kW charging and V2L

Perhaps the most attention-grabbing specification is the charging rate: the i3 supports peak DC charging at 400 kW, which BMW says can add approximately 400 kilometers of range in just 10 minutes. For comparison, the current BMW iX can charge at up to 195 kW and Tesla's Supercharger V4 network peaks at 250 kW, making the i3's charging capability significantly faster than what most rival platforms currently offer.

The car also adds vehicle-to-load (V2L) bidirectional charging at 3.7 kW — a feature BMW has not offered on any previous electric model. V2L allows owners to draw power from the car's battery pack to run appliances, charge devices, or supply a home or campsite.

Neue Klasse architecture

The i3 is built on the same Neue Klasse platform as the iX3, BMW's first Neue Klasse vehicle. The platform uses a new cell-to-pack integration approach and a sixth-generation eDrive powertrain with two electric motors — one per axle — producing a combined 463 horsepower and 476 pound-feet of torque, enabling 0-to-60 mph in well under 4 seconds.

The car also integrates Amazon Alexa+ AI as its infotainment assistant and features a native NACS (North American Charging Standard) charge port, granting access to Tesla's Supercharger network in North America without an adapter.

Launch timeline

Production of the BMW i3 is scheduled to begin at BMW's Munich plant in August 2026. European customer deliveries are expected to follow in the fall. US deliveries are slated for early 2027, with pricing not yet announced for either market. The segment it targets — premium compact electric sedans — is one of the most competitive in the EV market, with the Tesla Model 3 holding a commanding global share that the i3 is positioned to directly challenge.
