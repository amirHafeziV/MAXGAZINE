BMW i Ventures Launches $300 Million Fund for Agentic and Physical AI Startups

BMW i Ventures announced a new $300 million fund targeting early-stage to Series B startups working on agentic AI, physical AI, industrial software, and supply chain technology across North America and Europe, bringing the corporate venture arm's total capital under management to $1.1 billion.

BMW i Ventures, the corporate venture capital arm of BMW Group, announced on June 29, 2026 a new $300 million fund dedicated to agentic AI, physical AI, industrial software, advanced materials, and supply chain technology. The new vehicle brings BMW i Ventures' total capital under management to $1.1 billion across its North American and European investment portfolio.

Investment Focus

The fund targets companies from early-stage through Series B — startups that have demonstrated product-market fit and need capital to scale manufacturing, enterprise deployment, or commercial partnerships. The two thematic priorities are physical AI, defined as systems that operate in the physical world through robotics, manufacturing automation, and autonomous vehicles, and agentic AI, defined as systems that perform complex multi-step tasks autonomously rather than requiring human input at each step.

The geographic scope covers North America and Europe, reflecting BMW's supply chain concentration and regulatory environment. Automotive-sector corporate funds increasingly prefer these two geographies for physical AI investments because the regulatory frameworks for autonomous systems and advanced manufacturing are most mature in these markets.

What It Signals About the Auto Industry's AI Strategy

The BMW i Ventures announcement reflects a broader strategic shift among Tier 1 automotive manufacturers. Rather than waiting for commercial AI vendors to deliver physical and agentic AI capabilities to the automotive sector on standard commercial terms, leading automakers are deploying corporate venture capital to secure early access, co-development rights, and strategic relationships with startups that could become critical suppliers.

The physical AI category that BMW i Ventures is targeting includes companies such as Wayve, Physical Intelligence, Figure AI, and Apptronik — startups in autonomous vehicle software, general-purpose robotics, and manufacturing AI that have attracted multi-billion-dollar valuations since 2024. The pattern these companies share: they go from concept to raising $50 million or more within two years, and they represent potential future tier-one suppliers or acquisition targets for automakers building next-generation manufacturing and mobility systems.

For the broader automotive sector, June 2026 marks an inflection point in AI capital deployment. BMW's $300 million commitment follows major manufacturing AI investments by General Motors, Ford, and Hyundai in the preceding quarters. The cumulative effect is a well-funded corporate venture ecosystem specifically targeting the two AI categories — physical and agentic — that automakers have concluded will define competitive differentiation in vehicle production and autonomous mobility over the next decade.
