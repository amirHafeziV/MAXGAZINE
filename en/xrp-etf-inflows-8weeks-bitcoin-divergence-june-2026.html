XRP ETFs Post 8-Week Inflow Streak While Bitcoin Funds Bleed Billions

XRP spot ETFs posted $22.99 million in net inflows last week, their eighth consecutive week of positive flows, even as Bitcoin ETFs recorded hundreds of millions in outflows and BTC struggled to hold the $60,000 level before recovering to $63,000.

XRP spot exchange-traded funds posted $22.99 million in net inflows during the week ending June 27, 2026, extending what is now an eight-week consecutive positive-flow streak. The persistent demand contrasts sharply with Bitcoin's ETF market, where spot funds recorded net outflows totaling hundreds of millions of dollars across the same period, pushing cumulative Bitcoin ETF outflows above $2.75 billion since mid-May.

Bitcoin's price fell below $60,000 intraday on June 25 — its lowest level since late 2024 — before staging a partial recovery to approximately $63,000 by June 29. The Relative Strength Index on Bitcoin's weekly chart sat below 30, indicating oversold conditions. XRP, by contrast, traded at $1.06 on June 29, with a 24-hour gain of 1.4%, showing relative resilience against the broader selloff.

What Is Driving the Divergence

Several factors explain the XRP ETF inflow persistence. Most significantly, Ripple's regulatory trajectory in 2026 has provided the kind of forward clarity that institutional allocators respond to. Ripple settled its long-running US Securities and Exchange Commission lawsuit in 2024, removing the legal overhang that had suppressed XRP's institutional adoption for years. In 2026, the company obtained an Electronic Money Institution license in Luxembourg in February and preliminary MiCA Crypto Asset Service Provider approval in June, signaling deep integration into regulated financial infrastructure. Investors in XRP ETFs may be treating XRP as a regulated-payments-asset play rather than a speculative crypto-market position.

Bitcoin's ETF outflows, meanwhile, reflect macro pressures specific to the current environment. US PCE inflation printed at 4.1% in May 2026 — a three-year high — increasing the probability of a prolonged Federal Reserve hold or additional tightening. Institutional capital that flowed into Bitcoin ETFs when the Fed appeared close to cutting rates is now exiting to higher-yielding alternatives, including US Treasuries and AI equity investments. BlackRock alone deposited approximately 2,700 BTC and 52,956 ETH to Coinbase in late June — transactions totaling roughly $257 million — consistent with institutional liquidation or rebalancing activity.

Solana also showed relative strength, trading at $74.93 on June 29, up 4.69% on the day. The altcoin divergence from Bitcoin is a recurring pattern: when Bitcoin enters a prolonged drawdown driven by macro forces rather than crypto-specific events, tokens with clear regulatory catalysts or protocol-level momentum tend to hold up better against the broader pressure.
