About

Ondo (ONDO) is the governance token of Ondo Finance, a platform that specializes in bringing real-world assets (RWAs) — such as U.S. Treasuries and other traditional financial instruments — onto public blockchains. Instead of creating purely speculative crypto products, Ondo focuses on tokenizing established, yield-bearing assets so they can be held, traded and settled on-chain around the clock, with much of the compliance and structuring handled by the project.

Tokenized Treasuries and yield

Ondo's flagship products are tokenized representations of short-term U.S. government debt. OUSG offers institutional investors exposure to short-term Treasuries with rapid minting and redemption, while USDY is a yield-bearing token, backed by short-term Treasuries and bank deposits, whose value is designed to accrue over time. Unlike a typical stablecoin that pays no interest to holders, USDY passes the underlying yield through to the token itself, making it a way to hold a dollar-denominated asset that earns a return tied to prevailing Treasury rates. These products target both institutions seeking on-chain cash management and individual users looking for transparent, asset-backed yield.

The ONDO token

The ONDO token is used for governance of the Ondo ecosystem, giving holders a voice in protocol decisions through the Ondo DAO. It is distinct from the tokenized assets themselves: OUSG and USDY represent claims on real financial instruments, whereas ONDO is the coordinating token for the broader project and its community. This separation lets Ondo keep its regulated, asset-backed products distinct from the governance and incentive layer that steers the platform's direction.

A bridge between Wall Street and crypto

Ondo positions itself at the intersection of traditional finance and blockchain, often described as building "Wall Street on-chain." Its emphasis on compliance, institutional-grade structuring and partnerships with established financial players distinguishes it from more experimental DeFi protocols. As tokenization of real-world assets has grown into one of the most closely watched themes in crypto, Ondo has become a leading name, expanding from tokenized Treasuries toward a broader suite of on-chain financial products and the infrastructure needed to support them at scale.

History

Ondo Finance was founded in 2021 by Nathan Allman, a former member of Goldman Sachs's digital assets team, who set out to bring institutional-grade financial products onto blockchains. His traditional-finance background shaped the project's defining approach from day one: rather than chasing speculative yields, Ondo would tokenize familiar, regulated assets and emphasize compliance and professional structuring.

Early products and the RWA thesis

In its first years, Ondo experimented with several DeFi structures before sharpening its focus on real-world assets — the idea that traditional instruments like government bonds could be represented as blockchain tokens and made available to on-chain users. This led to its core offerings: OUSG, providing exposure to short-term U.S. Treasuries, and USDY, a yield-bearing, Treasury-backed token. As demand for transparent, asset-backed on-chain yield grew, Ondo's products gained traction with both crypto-native users and more traditional institutions.

The ONDO token launch

In January 2024, Ondo introduced its ONDO governance token, distributed in part through an airdrop, giving the community a stake in the project's direction via the Ondo DAO. The launch coincided with rising mainstream interest in tokenization and helped cement Ondo's position as one of the most prominent names in the RWA category, even as the team continued to expand its product lineup and the blockchains it supported.

Ondo Chain and ecosystem expansion

In February 2025, Ondo announced Ondo Chain, a proof-of-stake layer-1 blockchain purpose-built for institutional real-world assets, designed to provide the infrastructure to support tokenized securities at scale. The same period saw the launch of Ondo Global Markets, a platform for tokenized securities, and Ondo Nexus, aimed at improving liquidity and third-party issuance. The project's total value locked grew substantially, crossing multibillion-dollar levels, and a U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission investigation into Ondo was closed in late 2025 without charges, adding a measure of regulatory clarity.

A loss for the project

In May 2026, Ondo announced the unexpected passing of its founder, Nathan Allman, at the age of 32. Ian De Bode, the firm's longtime president, who had overseen strategy, product and day-to-day operations, stepped in as chief executive with the backing of the leadership team. Allman was widely credited with shaping Ondo into a pioneer of real-world-asset tokenization, and the transition marked a significant moment in the project's history as it continued to build on the foundation he had established.

Recent Developments

Ondo has established itself as one of the leading projects in the tokenization of real-world assets, a theme that has moved from the margins of crypto toward the center of how traditional finance and blockchains intersect.

Scaling tokenized finance

The project's total value locked has grown into the billions of dollars, reflecting sustained inflows into its Treasury-backed products rather than mere price appreciation. OUSG and USDY have expanded across multiple blockchains, and newer initiatives — including Ondo Global Markets for tokenized securities and Ondo Nexus for liquidity and third-party issuance — extend the platform beyond Treasuries toward a broader on-chain capital-markets offering. The ambition, often summarized as building "Wall Street on-chain," is to let tokenized stocks, bonds and funds trade with the speed and accessibility of crypto while retaining institutional-grade structure.

Infrastructure and Ondo Chain

Ondo Chain, the project's purpose-built layer-1 for institutional RWAs, is central to this strategy. By providing dedicated infrastructure for compliant tokenized assets, Ondo aims to support the scale and reliability that institutions require, positioning itself not just as an issuer of tokenized products but as a provider of the rails on which such assets can operate.

Regulatory and market context

The closing of a U.S. SEC investigation without charges in late 2025 gave the project a measure of regulatory clarity that risk-conscious institutions value. As governments and regulators worldwide develop frameworks for tokenized securities and stablecoins, Ondo's compliance-first approach positions it to benefit if real-world-asset tokenization continues to gain mainstream acceptance — though, as with any project, regulatory outcomes remain uncertain.

Outlook

Following the 2026 leadership transition after the death of founder Nathan Allman, Ondo has continued to pursue its roadmap under new chief executive Ian De Bode. Its future will hinge on sustained demand for tokenized yield, the success of Ondo Chain and Global Markets, and the broader trajectory of RWA adoption. With a clear focus and a leading position in one of crypto's most closely watched categories, ONDO remains a prominent name to follow — though nothing here should be taken as financial advice.
