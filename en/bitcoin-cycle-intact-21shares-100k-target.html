21Shares: Bitcoin's Four-Year Cycle Holds Intact Despite 50% Drawdown, $100K Year-End Target Maintained

Asset manager 21Shares has trimmed its broader 2026 crypto forecasts but held its base-case Bitcoin target of $100,000 by year-end, arguing that the current ~50% pullback from October 2025's $126,000 peak is tracking historical post-halving patterns more closely than many analysts expected.

In a research note published on June 26, 2026, 21Shares cut its 2026 crypto forecasts across several metrics — citing slower-than-expected enterprise adoption, a record DeFi hack season, and persistent ETF outflows — but maintained its Bitcoin base case of $100,000 by December 2026.

Why the Cycle Remains Intact

The core argument in 21Shares' analysis is that Bitcoin's four-year halving cycle, which many institutional commentators expected to weaken as the asset class matured, remains structurally intact. Bitcoin peaked near $126,000 in October 2025 before entering a correction. As of late June 2026, BTC was trading near $60,000 — a drawdown of roughly 52% from peak.

Historically, Bitcoin has experienced drawdowns of 80% or more in post-halving bear phases. The current correction, 21Shares argues, is comparatively shallow and has not violated its aggregate cost basis of approximately $54,000 — the average price at which all BTC in circulation was last moved. Every time Bitcoin has dropped toward cost basis and held, it has recovered strongly within 12 to 18 months.

"The halving cycle is evolving, but it is not broken," the firm wrote. "Institutional participation has moderated how violently markets draw down, but the fundamental cyclical behavior — peak, correction, base, recovery — has not been disrupted."

What 21Shares Revised Down

The firm was more cautious on the broader crypto market. It revised down its forecasts for DeFi total value locked (TVL), citing a record second quarter for exploits: more than 70 incidents and roughly $746 million stolen, the highest single-quarter count ever. It also trimmed expectations for institutional crypto treasury allocations and on-chain transaction volumes for altcoins, noting that ETF outflows for BTC, ETH, SOL, and XRP extended to 13 consecutive sessions in early June.

On the positive side, 21Shares highlighted prediction markets as one of crypto's strongest growth areas, projecting annual trading volume will exceed $100 billion in 2026.

The Institutional Thesis Under Scrutiny

A key question for the second half of 2026 is whether institutional investors will re-enter crypto markets after a sustained exit. According to 21Shares, institutional ownership of Bitcoin fell roughly 17% in the first quarter of 2026, and ETF outflows have been the dominant story since late April.

For the cycle to play out as 21Shares expects — a recovery toward $100,000 by December — Bitcoin likely needs at least one of the following: a Federal Reserve pivot signal, a resolution of the US regulatory uncertainty (particularly around the GENIUS Act stablecoin framework), or a significant macro risk-off event that repositions Bitcoin as a store of value rather than a risk asset.

Where Bitcoin Stands

Bitcoin's current price of roughly $60,000 represents the lower bound of what 21Shares considers a viable recovery range. If BTC breaks below its aggregate cost basis of $54,000, the firm says it would revisit its year-end target. For now, the base case holds: cycle intact, institutions slower but not absent, and $100,000 remains in view by December 2026.
