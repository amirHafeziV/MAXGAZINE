CLARITY Act's July 4 Deadline: Senate Needs 7 More Democrats and 31 Session Days to Rewrite Crypto Law

The White House has set July 4 as its target date for the crypto market structure bill. But with the Senate needing 60 votes and bipartisan talks stuck on a developer liability clause, the clock is ticking loudly.

Published 2026-06-17 · MAXGAZINE Desk

The Digital Asset Market Clarity Act — known as the CLARITY Act — is now on the Senate Legislative Calendar after a 15-9 committee vote on June 1, 2026, setting up what could be the most consequential crypto regulatory vote in US history. But getting it to the floor is proving more complicated than the strong House passage (294-134 in July 2025) might suggest.

The 60-Vote Problem

In the Senate, the bill must clear a filibuster threshold of 60 votes. Republican leadership counts 53 dependable votes; reaching 60 requires at least seven Democrats beyond the two — Senators Mark Warner and Catherine Cortez Masto — who have expressed openness but stopped short of committing.

The sticking point is Section 604, which protects crypto software developers from money-transmitter regulations. Law enforcement agencies, particularly the DOJ and FinCEN, have raised objections, and the two uncommitted Democrats say they cannot vote yes until those concerns are resolved. Bipartisan negotiators are reported to be in daily talks to find legislative language that satisfies both sides.

The July 4 and August Recess Clocks

White House crypto adviser Patrick Witt has publicly set July 4, 2026 as the target date, arguing it is the last realistic window before the 2026 midterm election cycle hardens partisan lines. With roughly 31 Senate session days remaining before the August recess, floor time is scarce: the bill is estimated to require up to a full week of debate — one of the most valuable commodities in a packed legislative calendar.

If the bill does not pass before the recess, the window narrows sharply. After August, the Senate returns for only a short stretch before midterm positioning takes over, and crypto legislation becomes politically riskier for members facing swing-district pressure.

What Passage Would Mean

The CLARITY Act would establish a formal division of oversight between the SEC and the CFTC for digital assets, with most tokens — including XRP — classified as commodities under CFTC jurisdiction. For the crypto industry, the bill's passage would end years of regulatory ambiguity that has pushed exchanges and developers offshore.

Prediction markets currently assign a 59–72% probability to the bill becoming law by the end of 2026, reflecting both the genuine momentum behind it and the real risk that a scheduling crunch kills it for another year.
