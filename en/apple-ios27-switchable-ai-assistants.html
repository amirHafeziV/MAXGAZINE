Apple's iOS 27 Turns Siri Into a Marketplace: How Claude, Gemini, and ChatGPT Can Now Replace Your iPhone's AI

At WWDC 2026 on June 9, Apple unveiled iOS 27's AI Extensions system, allowing users to designate Claude, Gemini, or ChatGPT as their default AI across iPhone, replacing Siri for most functions — a move that rewrites the competitive dynamics of the AI assistant market and gives Anthropic its first native iPhone distribution channel.

Apple's Worldwide Developers Conference on June 9, 2026 delivered what many analysts consider the most consequential announcement for the AI industry in 2026: iOS 27's AI Extensions system, which transforms Siri from a single assistant into a platform through which users can configure any major AI model as their device's default intelligence layer.

What Apple Actually Announced

The core change is architectural. In iOS 27, Apple has added an "Extensions" section to the Apple Intelligence settings in iPhone Settings. From there, users can select from a list of supported AI providers — initially including Anthropic's Claude, Google's Gemini, and OpenAI's ChatGPT — and designate one as the default assistant for queries Siri cannot handle natively.

This is distinct from Apple's existing ChatGPT integration in iOS 18, which was a single-provider hand-off with explicit user permission required per session. Under iOS 27's Extensions model, the chosen AI can respond seamlessly to voice queries, handle file attachments, and assist with Apple Intelligence features — making the experience closer to a native integration than a referral.

Apple also announced that Siri itself will be powered by a licensed 1.2-trillion-parameter Gemini model from Google, reportedly at approximately $1 billion per year. This makes Apple simultaneously a distribution platform for competing AI products and a Google AI customer.

Why This Matters for the AI Industry

Apple ships roughly 200 million iPhones per year globally. The AI Extensions marketplace, if rolled out to all iOS 27 users, would give Claude, Gemini, and ChatGPT access to a combined addressable audience of more than a billion active iPhones — without each company needing to drive users to a standalone app.

For Anthropic, the implications are specifically significant. Claude has gained wide adoption among enterprise users and developers, but its consumer retail presence has lagged behind ChatGPT. A native Siri Extensions slot on iOS 27 represents the most direct path Anthropic has had to consumer devices at scale.

For Google, the arrangement is structurally unusual: the company is simultaneously powering Siri's core AI and competing against Siri as an Extensions provider through its standalone Gemini product.

What Users Can Actually Do

Under iOS 27, a user can:

  • Set Claude as the default assistant for complex multi-step tasks
  • Use Gemini for real-time search and Google account integration
  • Switch to ChatGPT for creative or coding assistance
  • Return to native Siri for Apple device control functions (calls, messages, HomeKit, etc.)

The Extensions system does not give third-party AIs access to Apple's proprietary on-device processing or the secure enclave. Privacy-sensitive tasks still default to Apple's native layer.

The Competitive Shift

The AI assistant market has historically been shaped by platform lock-in: Google Assistant on Android, Siri on Apple, Cortana (briefly) on Windows. iOS 27's Extensions model represents a significant departure — Apple is essentially conceding that no single AI is superior for all tasks and creating a multi-model framework instead.

This mirrors patterns seen in the early mobile app store era, when device manufacturers moved from bundled applications to open marketplaces. The analogy is imperfect — Apple maintains significant controls over which AIs qualify for Extensions and sets policies on what data they can access — but the structural direction is consistent.

For users across all four MAXGAZINE markets — the US, Persian-speaking world, Arab countries, and Turkey — this shift has practical implications: all three AI models (Claude, Gemini, ChatGPT) have multilingual capabilities and support non-English interfaces, making the choice genuinely competitive for non-English speakers on iPhones for the first time.
