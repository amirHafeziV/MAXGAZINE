About

Venice Token (VVV) is the native token of Venice AI, a decentralized artificial intelligence platform built on the Base blockchain (Coinbase's Layer-2 network on Ethereum). Venice AI offers private AI inference — the ability to run AI models such as large language models and image generators — with a design guarantee that user prompts and AI outputs are never stored, logged, or used for training. The platform positions itself as a privacy-first alternative to centralized AI services like ChatGPT or Claude, where user data may be retained and used to improve the service.

How VVV Works

The VVV token provides holders with access to Venice's compute capacity through a mechanism called Venice Compute Units (VCUs). When a user stakes VVV, they receive a proportional share of the platform's total inference capacity in the form of VCUs, which can be used to make AI requests or can be rented out to other users who need more capacity than their own stake provides.

This design makes VVV a productive asset: stakers are not merely speculating on price appreciation, but are also receiving a flow of usable compute resources that have a direct real-world application. Users who need AI inference but do not hold VVV can purchase access directly, and the revenue from those purchases is distributed back to VVV stakers, creating a yield dynamic tied to actual platform usage.

Supported Models

Venice AI runs open-source AI models that can be deployed without requiring proprietary access or licensing from centralized AI companies. These include models from the Llama family (Meta's open-weight models), Mistral, and various image generation models. Because these models are open-source, Venice can run them on its own infrastructure without any data-sharing agreement with model developers, supporting the platform's privacy architecture.

History

Venice AI was founded by Erik Voorhees, a well-known figure in the cryptocurrency industry who previously founded ShapeShift, a non-custodial cryptocurrency exchange that was at one point among the most widely used crypto-to-crypto trading platforms before the broader market shifted toward centralized exchanges and KYC-compliant services.

Founding Vision

Voorhees' founding of Venice AI followed a pattern consistent with his long-held views on privacy and individual sovereignty. His experience at ShapeShift, which transitioned from a permissionless, account-free exchange to a KYC-compliant platform under regulatory pressure, shaped his interest in building products that could provide strong privacy guarantees at the infrastructure level rather than relying on operator discretion.

Venice AI was designed with the view that as AI becomes a primary tool for personal productivity, research, and communication, the privacy of what users ask AI systems and what those systems return will become increasingly important. The platform was built around open-source models specifically so that neither Venice as an operator nor any upstream model developer would have a technical mechanism to access user data.

VVV Token Launch in 2025

The VVV token launched in early 2025, with an initial distribution that included an airdrop to early Venice AI users and a public sale component. The launch attracted significant attention from both the crypto community and the emerging crypto-AI intersection community — a group interested in the combination of decentralized infrastructure and AI capabilities.

Following the token launch, VVV rapidly rose into the top tiers of cryptocurrency market capitalization, driven by enthusiasm for the AI crypto narrative and the platform's clear value proposition around privacy. The rapid appreciation brought substantial liquidity to the token on major decentralized and centralized exchanges.

Recent Developments

Venice AI has grown rapidly since the VVV token launch and the broader crypto market's enthusiasm for AI-related projects in 2025. The platform has expanded its range of supported AI models, added new features including document processing and code generation capabilities, and grown its user base beyond the initial crypto-native early adopters.

Ecosystem and Integrations

Developers building on Base and other EVM-compatible networks have begun integrating Venice's private AI capabilities into their own applications, using Venice's API to provide AI functionality without inheriting the data-retention practices of centralized AI providers. This developer ecosystem adoption is an important growth channel for Venice: each application that integrates Venice creates new sources of demand for VCUs and, by extension, for VVV staking.

The platform's relationship with the Base blockchain has positioned it within Coinbase's growing on-chain economy, where a range of consumer and developer applications have been built since Base's launch.

Privacy as a Market Position

Venice's core differentiator — private AI inference where no data is retained — is both its greatest strength and a characteristic that creates constraints. Because Venice does not retain conversation history, it cannot offer the kind of persistent memory and long-context features that users of centralized AI tools like ChatGPT have come to expect. This trade-off is intentional: Venice's target user is someone who specifically values the privacy guarantee over conversational continuity.

The long-term success of Venice AI and VVV will depend on the breadth of demand for private AI inference relative to the total AI market, how well the platform's compute capacity can scale to meet user demand, and whether the open-source models Venice runs can keep pace with the capability improvements of proprietary frontier models from major AI labs.
