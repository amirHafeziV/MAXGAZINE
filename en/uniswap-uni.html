About

Uniswap (UNI) is the governance token of the Uniswap Protocol, the largest decentralized exchange by historical trading volume and one of the most influential protocols in the history of decentralized finance. Uniswap allows any user to swap ERC-20 tokens directly from a self-custodied wallet, provide liquidity to earn fees, or create new trading pairs — all without accounts, KYC, or a central intermediary.

The AMM Model

Uniswap pioneered the Automated Market Maker (AMM) design in which a mathematical formula — rather than an order book of buyers and sellers — determines token prices. The original Uniswap formula, x*y=k, means the product of the two tokens in a liquidity pool must remain constant. When a trader swaps one token for the other, the formula automatically adjusts the price based on the change in pool composition. Liquidity is provided by users who deposit token pairs into pools and receive a share of the trading fees generated by swaps.

This model eliminated the need for market makers, order matching engines, and centralized custody — replacing all of them with a smart contract and a simple invariant. The AMM design has since been adopted and iterated upon by hundreds of DeFi protocols.

Protocol Versions

Uniswap has iterated significantly since its launch. Uniswap v1 (2018) supported only ETH-to-token swaps. Uniswap v2 (2020) introduced token-to-token pools and flash swaps. Uniswap v3 (2021) introduced concentrated liquidity, allowing liquidity providers to specify price ranges, dramatically improving capital efficiency. Uniswap v4 (2024) introduced hooks — a plugin architecture that allows developers to add custom logic to pools at initialization, before/after swaps, and at other lifecycle points — enabling far more sophisticated pool designs without forking the core protocol.

UNI Token and Governance

The UNI token is the governance token of the Uniswap Protocol, giving holders the ability to vote on protocol upgrades, fee switches, grant programs, and treasury deployments. Uniswap's treasury holds a significant amount of UNI and ETH, making governance decisions consequential. The protocol is governed by Uniswap DAO, with proposals requiring a quorum of voting UNI to pass.

History

Uniswap was created by Hayden Adams, a mechanical engineering graduate who had recently been laid off from Siemens in 2017. After learning about Ethereum from a friend, Adams became fascinated by the idea of building financial products as open-source code on a public blockchain. A blog post by Ethereum co-founder Vitalik Buterin describing an on-chain market maker provided the conceptual seed for what would become Uniswap.

v1: Proof of Concept (2018)

Adams received a $65,000 grant from the Ethereum Foundation and built Uniswap v1 largely alone. The protocol launched on Ethereum mainnet in November 2018. v1 was limited: it could only swap between ETH and individual ERC-20 tokens (not token-to-token directly), and the interface was minimal. Despite its constraints, v1 demonstrated that AMM-based exchange was viable and attracted several million dollars in liquidity within months.

v2: Token-to-Token and Flash Loans (2020)

Uniswap v2 launched in May 2020, adding direct ERC-20 to ERC-20 trading pairs, time-weighted average price (TWAP) oracles that became widely used across DeFi, and flash swaps — the ability to borrow any amount of tokens from a pool within a single transaction as long as they are returned by the transaction's end. The Uniswap team, by then operating as Uniswap Labs, also raised a $11 million Series A from Andreessen Horowitz.

The UNI Airdrop (September 2020)

In September 2020, Uniswap launched the UNI governance token and executed one of crypto history's most celebrated airdrops: 400 UNI tokens distributed to every address that had ever interacted with the protocol. At the time, this represented hundreds of dollars per recipient; at later price peaks, the airdrop was worth thousands. The airdrop served both as a community-building mechanism and as a response to Sushiswap — a fork that had attempted a vampire attack by migrating Uniswap's liquidity.

v3: Concentrated Liquidity (2021)

Uniswap v3 launched in May 2021 and introduced concentrated liquidity, the most significant innovation in AMM design since Uniswap v1. Rather than spreading liquidity uniformly across all prices from zero to infinity, LPs could specify a price range, concentrating capital where trading was most likely to occur. This dramatically improved capital efficiency — a given amount of capital could generate many multiples more in fee revenue — but also made LP position management more active and complex.

v4: Hooks and Multichain (2024)

Uniswap v4 launched in 2024 with hooks — a programmable extension system allowing developers to add custom logic at key lifecycle points of a pool. Alongside v4, Uniswap expanded its deployment across multiple layer-2 networks and launched UniswapX, a cross-chain swap aggregation protocol using a Dutch auction mechanism to source best execution from on-chain and off-chain liquidity.

Recent Developments

By mid-2026, Uniswap has maintained its position as one of the leading decentralized exchanges despite intense competition from both protocol competitors and vertically integrated chains. The v4 architecture, with its flexible hook system, has enabled a new generation of specialized pool designs built on top of Uniswap's core liquidity infrastructure.

v4 Ecosystem

Hooks have proven to be a significant differentiator. Developers have built on-chain limit orders, dynamic fee tiers, TWAP-based liquidity concentration, MEV protection mechanisms, and KYC-gated pools using the hook interface — all without requiring changes to the core Uniswap protocol. This extensibility has attracted builders who previously would have forked Uniswap to add custom functionality, reducing fragmentation of liquidity.

Multi-Chain Expansion

Uniswap's deployment across Ethereum mainnet, Optimism, Arbitrum, Base, Polygon, and other layer-2 and EVM-compatible networks has diversified its volume base significantly. A growing share of Uniswap's total trading volume now occurs on layer-2 networks where gas fees are minimal, making it accessible for smaller trades that would be uneconomical on Ethereum mainnet.

UNI Governance

The Uniswap DAO has continued to debate the activation of protocol fee switches — the ability to redirect a portion of swap fees from liquidity providers to UNI token holders. This question, central to UNI's long-term value proposition, remained a subject of active governance discussion heading into 2026, with various proposals having been tested on individual chains before any broader deployment. The protocol's treasury and the maturity of its governance process position Uniswap as one of the more consequential DAO experiments in decentralized finance.
