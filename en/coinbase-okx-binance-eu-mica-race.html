Coinbase and OKX Race to Capture Binance's 450 Million EU Users Before MiCA Deadline

With Binance set to suspend EU services on July 1 after failing to secure a MiCA license, Coinbase is offering a 5% transfer bonus and OKX an 8% deposit match to pull in European crypto users—the biggest scramble for market share in European crypto history.

Two of the world's largest licensed crypto exchanges are racing to absorb Binance's European user base as the July 1, 2026 MiCA deadline forces the market's longtime dominant player to halt key services in the European Union. Coinbase and OKX launched competing transfer-incentive campaigns on June 27, offering cash bonuses to EU and UK users who migrate their funds before the window closes.

Coinbase, which secured its MiCA license in 2025, is offering a 5% transfer bonus to new signups in Germany, France, Italy, Belgium, Poland, Sweden, and the United Kingdom for funds moved to the platform before July 13. OKX is running an 8% deposit-match campaign covering all 27 EU member states plus Iceland, Norway, and Liechtenstein, running from June 12 to July 13.

Why Binance Lost Its MiCA Window

Binance, which serves an estimated 450 million registered users globally with a significant share in Europe, was unable to meet the Markets in Crypto-Assets regulation's licensing requirements by the July 1 transition deadline. MiCA, the EU's comprehensive crypto asset framework that entered full force in late 2024, requires exchanges to hold a formal authorization in at least one EU member state to offer services across the bloc. Binance announced it would suspend spot trading, staking, and derivatives access for EU residents unable to migrate to an authorized regional entity.

The scale of the potential customer migration is unprecedented in European crypto. Neither Coinbase nor OKX disclosed the size of Binance's addressable EU user pool, but analysts at major trading platforms have estimated it at 40 to 60 million active users across the continent.

For Coinbase, the EU push is part of a broader international expansion strategy following its successful IPO trajectory and the passage of the CLARITY Act in the United States. For OKX, the European campaign marks an aggressive bid to establish a top-tier presence in a region where it has historically been a secondary player behind Binance and Coinbase.

Industry observers note that the competitive intensity of the bonuses—at 5% and 8% of transferred or deposited funds respectively—reflects how transformative the MiCA transition could be for European market structure, potentially reshuffling exchange rankings that have been largely stable for several years.
