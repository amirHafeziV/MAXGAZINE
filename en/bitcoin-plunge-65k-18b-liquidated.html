Bitcoin Plunges Below $65K as $1.8 Billion in Leveraged Trades Liquidated

Bitcoin fell below $65,000 in early June 2026, triggering over $1.8 billion in liquidations as crypto markets entered sharp correction territory.

Published MAXGAZINE Desk · 2026-06-14
AD SPACEIn-article bannerBook this spot →

The Flash Crash That Shook Crypto Markets

Bitcoin dropped sharply below $65,000 in the first week of June 2026, triggering a wave of forced liquidations that erased over $1.8 billion in leveraged positions in a single day. The selloff caught overleveraged traders off guard, as cascading stop-losses and margin calls amplified the initial decline into a full-scale market rout.

The move was particularly severe on derivatives platforms, where open interest had been building to elevated levels through late May. As prices broke key technical support zones, automated liquidation engines fired across exchanges simultaneously, turning an orderly pullback into a panic. Data from on-chain analytics firms showed Bitcoin's Realized Volatility spiking to its highest point since late 2024.

What Triggered the Selloff

Analysts pointed to several converging factors. Macro headwinds played a role, with the U.S. dollar strengthening following stronger-than-expected employment data. At the same time, concerns about geopolitical tensions — particularly involving disruptions to oil supply through the Strait of Hormuz — pushed risk-off sentiment across global markets, spilling into digital assets.

Strategy's Michael Saylor, whose firm holds the largest known corporate Bitcoin treasury, hinted at a potential resumption of purchases after the company controversially broke its long-standing "never sell" policy last week — a move that rattled market confidence in the institutional holding narrative.

Market Reaction and Outlook

Despite the violent correction, analysts at several crypto research firms characterized the move as a demand-driven pullback rather than a structural breakdown. Bitcoin's 2026 correction, while significant, remains shallower than the 2022 bear market by most metrics. The Fear & Greed index dropped into Extreme Fear territory, a zone that has historically preceded recoveries.

Bitcoin dominance held at 58%, suggesting altcoins suffered even more severe drawdowns, with many mid-cap tokens losing 20–35% over the same period. Ethereum fell over 18% from its recent highs. Traders are now watching the $60,000 psychological support level as the next critical battleground for bulls.
