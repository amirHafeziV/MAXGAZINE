About

Mantle (MNT) is a layer-2 network built to scale Ethereum, making transactions faster and cheaper while still settling on the security of the Ethereum mainnet. It uses a rollup design, processing transactions off the main chain and posting the results back to Ethereum, and pairs this with a modular architecture that separates the network's functions into distinct layers. MNT is the network's native token, used to pay fees and to participate in governance.

Modular rollup design

Most Ethereum layer-2s bundle execution, settlement and data availability together. Mantle instead embraces a modular approach, decoupling data availability — the job of making sure transaction data is published and verifiable — from the core rollup. Mantle's data-availability layer has drawn on EigenLayer's restaking infrastructure, allowing the network to handle large volumes of data more cheaply than posting everything directly to Ethereum. This separation is designed to lower fees and increase throughput while keeping the ledger verifiable by independent participants.

The MNT token and on-chain treasury

MNT is used to pay transaction fees on the network and to vote on governance proposals that shape Mantle's direction. It has a capped maximum supply in the billions of tokens, with a portion still entering circulation over time. A distinctive feature of the project is its very large on-chain treasury — one of the biggest in the industry — which funds ecosystem growth, liquidity programs and new products. This treasury, inherited from the project's origins, gives Mantle unusual financial firepower to bootstrap applications and incentives.

Liquid staking and an expanding product suite

Beyond the base network, Mantle has built out a broader financial stack. Its liquid staking product lets users stake ETH and receive mETH, a token representing their staked position that can be used elsewhere in decentralized finance. Over time the project has expanded toward a wider "on-chain finance" vision, exploring products such as index funds, banking-style services and tools that connect traditional finance with on-chain rails. Taken together, Mantle aims to be more than a generic scaling solution: a capital-rich ecosystem combining a modular layer-2 with a suite of financial applications built on top of it.

History

Mantle's roots lie in BitDAO, one of the largest decentralized autonomous organizations of its era. BitDAO was established in 2021, with much of its funding linked to the cryptocurrency exchange Bybit, and it amassed a substantial treasury aimed at funding crypto projects and ecosystems. Out of this organization came the idea for an Ethereum scaling network.

From BitDAO to Mantle

Mantle began in June 2022 as a layer-2 project sponsored by BitDAO, with a testnet following in January 2023. In May 2023, a BitDAO governance proposal passed with overwhelming support to consolidate the organization under a single identity: BitDAO would become Mantle, and its BIT token would be converted into MNT, under the slogan "One brand, One token." The Mantle mainnet launched soon after, in July 2023, turning what had been a DAO-backed initiative into a live network with its own token and one of the largest treasuries in the space.

Building the technology stack

From the start, Mantle distinguished itself with a modular design, separating data availability from execution and settlement. Rather than posting all transaction data directly to Ethereum — the most expensive part of running a rollup — Mantle used a dedicated data-availability layer drawing on EigenLayer's restaking technology, helping keep fees low. In September 2023 the project launched its liquid staking protocol, allowing users to stake ETH and receive mETH, broadening Mantle from a pure scaling chain into a platform with its own financial primitives.

Mantle 2.0 and deeper Bybit ties

Through 2024 and 2025, Mantle expanded its ecosystem and refined its architecture. The rollout of Mantle 2.0 coincided with closer strategic alignment with Bybit, including the appointment of senior Bybit executives as advisers in 2025 — a reflection of the exchange's continued backing dating back to the BitDAO era. This alliance aimed to bring Mantle's roadmap and Bybit's reach closer together, blending decentralized and centralized finance.

Toward a ZK future

In September 2025, Mantle announced plans to upgrade its mainnet toward a zero-knowledge (ZK) validity rollup, transitioning away from the optimistic rollup model it had used. ZK proofs allow a network to prove the correctness of its transactions cryptographically, which can shorten withdrawal times and strengthen security guarantees. This planned shift placed Mantle among the layer-2 projects moving toward what many see as the long-term direction for Ethereum scaling.

Recent Developments

Mantle today is positioned as more than a single layer-2: it combines a modular scaling network, an unusually large on-chain treasury and a growing suite of financial products, all under the MNT token.

A capital-rich ecosystem

Mantle's defining advantage is its treasury, among the largest controlled by any crypto project. This war chest funds liquidity incentives, grants and product development, giving the ecosystem the resources to bootstrap applications that might struggle to gain traction elsewhere. The project has used this capital to push beyond basic scaling toward an "on-chain finance" vision, exploring index products, banking-style services and tools that connect users to both decentralized and traditional financial markets.

Technology roadmap

The planned move toward a ZK validity rollup is one of the most significant items on Mantle's roadmap. Transitioning from an optimistic to a zero-knowledge model can reduce the long waiting periods associated with withdrawals and strengthen the network's security proofs, aligning Mantle with where much of the Ethereum scaling field is heading. Combined with its modular data-availability design, this positions Mantle to compete on both cost and security as the layer-2 landscape matures.

Bybit alignment and liquidity

Mantle's close ties with Bybit, dating back to the BitDAO era and reinforced by advisory appointments in 2025, give it access to liquidity, distribution and exchange infrastructure that many independent layer-2s lack. This relationship blends centralized and decentralized finance, though it also ties part of Mantle's fortunes to a single large exchange — a dynamic worth understanding for anyone following the project.

Outlook

Mantle competes in a crowded field of Ethereum layer-2 networks, where success depends on attracting developers, applications and users. Its differentiators — a modular architecture, a massive treasury and an expanding financial product suite — give it tools that few rivals can match, but the broader challenge of standing out among many scaling solutions remains. For those tracking the evolution of Ethereum scaling and on-chain finance, MNT is a notable token to watch, though nothing here constitutes financial advice.
