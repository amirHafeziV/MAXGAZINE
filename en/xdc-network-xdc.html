About

XDC Network is an enterprise-grade, EVM-compatible hybrid blockchain developed with a specific focus on trade finance, cross-border payments and supply chain management. It is designed to serve both public blockchain use cases and private, permissioned environments, combining the transparency advantages of a public chain with the control and privacy options that enterprises require when handling sensitive commercial transactions.

The network uses a consensus mechanism called XDPoS (XinFin Delegated Proof of Stake), which is designed for high transaction throughput and near-instant finality. XDPoS relies on a set of masternode validators who stake XDC tokens to participate in consensus, enabling the network to process a high volume of transactions at very low cost — characteristics that make it practical for financial applications that may involve large numbers of transactions per day.

XDC's smart contract environment is compatible with Ethereum's EVM, meaning existing Solidity tools and code can be ported to the network with minimal modifications. This compatibility has been an important factor in attracting developers and financial technology companies building trade-finance automation tools on the blockchain.

The network has been developed with alignment to international financial standards, including ISO 20022, the global standard for financial messaging used by banks and payment systems worldwide. This compatibility is intended to make it easier to integrate XDC-based applications with existing banking infrastructure and cross-border payment rails, lowering the adoption barriers for financial institutions considering blockchain solutions for trade and settlement.

History

XDC Network was developed by XinFin (XinFin Digital Contract), a Singapore-based financial technology company founded to apply distributed ledger technology to real-world trade and finance problems. The project originated around 2017-2018, during the period of broad experimentation with enterprise blockchain applications, when financial institutions and trade finance companies were actively exploring how blockchain could streamline letters of credit, trade documentation and cross-border payments.

The XDC mainnet launched in 2019, positioning the network as a production-ready infrastructure for financial applications with a focus on ISO 20022 messaging standards and interoperability with existing banking systems. The choice to build as a hybrid blockchain — with both a public network and the option for enterprises to run private subnets — was deliberate: it allowed institutional users to maintain control over sensitive data while still benefiting from the settlement finality and auditability of the public chain.

TradeFinex, a platform for peer-to-peer trade finance and invoice financing built on XDC, became one of the earliest and most prominent applications on the network, showcasing how small and medium enterprises could access supply chain financing using tokenized trade instruments on the blockchain. TradeFinex was conceived as a direct demonstration of XDC's intended use case — reducing the cost and friction of trade finance for businesses that historically struggled to access it through traditional banking channels.

The network built partnerships with various organizations in the financial services and trade sector, and XDC tokens were adopted for use in several trade finance pilot programs, bringing practical validation to the network's technology claims.

Recent Developments

XDC Network continues to operate as an active blockchain with a focus on financial services and enterprise adoption. The network has maintained its position in the trade finance space, where it has established partnerships with various organizations pursuing the digitization of trade documentation, letters of credit and supply chain instruments.

The development community has continued to expand the XDC ecosystem with DeFi protocols, NFT platforms and other applications that leverage the network's low-cost, high-throughput characteristics. The EVM compatibility ensures that developers from the broader Ethereum ecosystem can bring their tools and experience to XDC with minimal friction, gradually widening the range of applications available on the network.

XDC's alignment with ISO 20022 has remained a key differentiator as the global financial system has gradually shifted toward this standard for inter-bank and cross-border payment messaging. This positioning makes XDC relevant to conversations about how blockchain technology can integrate with — rather than replace — existing financial infrastructure. For investors and observers, XDC represents a bet on enterprise blockchain adoption in trade finance and payments: a market that has been slower to adopt than consumer crypto but that carries significant long-term volume potential given the trillions of dollars in annual global trade.
