BitMine's Race to Own 5% of Ethereum Signals a New Era for Corporate Crypto Treasuries

Tom Lee's BitMine now holds more than 5.5 million ETH — nearly 4.5% of the circulating supply — after its largest single weekly purchase of 2026. The company's trajectory mirrors Michael Saylor's Bitcoin playbook, raising big questions about what concentrated institutional ownership means for Ethereum.

Published 2026-06-07 · MAXGAZINE Desk

On June 7, 2026, BitMine Immersion Technologies disclosed that it had purchased 126,971 ETH — worth approximately $214 million at prevailing prices — making it the company's largest single-week accumulation of 2026. The purchase brought BitMine's total holdings to over 5.54 million ETH, representing roughly 4.4% of Ethereum's entire circulating supply. BitMine's publicly stated goal is to reach 5%.

Tom Lee, the firm's chairman and a longtime market strategist, described the purchase as an expression of confidence in Ethereum fundamentals during what he called a geopolitically driven market pullback. "We increased our buying as we believe this pullback in ETH prices does not reflect the strengthening of Ethereum fundamentals," Lee said in a statement accompanying the disclosure.

From Bitcoin Mining to ETH Treasury

BitMine began life as a Bitcoin mining company before Lee orchestrated a strategic pivot in 2025 toward building an Ethereum treasury on a scale without precedent among publicly traded companies. The move was explicitly modeled on MicroStrategy (now Strategy), where Michael Saylor has spent years accumulating Bitcoin as the company's primary balance-sheet asset.

Strategy's playbook — raise capital through equity and convertible notes, deploy proceeds into the target asset, report treasury size as a key corporate metric — has been adopted almost identically by BitMine for Ethereum. Like Strategy with Bitcoin, BitMine has continued buying even during pronounced market drawdowns, treating volatility as an accumulation opportunity rather than a reason to pause.

Scale and Staking Yield

BitMine's position is notable not only for its size but for its income-generating characteristics. The company has staked more than 90% of its ETH holdings — over 4.7 million tokens — through its Made in America Validator Network (MAVAN). Ethereum's proof-of-stake system currently pays validators an annualized yield of approximately 4–5%, which for BitMine translates into a substantial ongoing return on its holdings, effectively functioning like an interest-bearing treasury instrument.

This staking component distinguishes the ETH treasury model from Bitcoin's. Bitcoin generates no native yield; holders of Strategy's BTC depend entirely on price appreciation. BitMine's ETH, by contrast, earns yield that compounds the position over time — a feature that Lee and the company have highlighted as a core argument for Ethereum over Bitcoin as a corporate treasury asset.

Concentration and Market Questions

The scale of BitMine's accumulation raises structural questions. With 4.4% of circulating ETH already held by a single company and a target of 5%, market observers have asked what concentrated single-entity ownership means for Ethereum's claimed decentralization. Ethereum's security model depends on a broad and distributed validator set; large staking concentrations — even by well-intentioned actors — create governance risk if a single entity controls a significant slice of the validator pool.

On the supply side, BitMine's continued large-scale buying has the effect of removing ETH from circulation and from active DeFi markets. In a market where ETH is simultaneously serving as collateral for DeFi protocols, as a fee-payment token for Ethereum transactions, and as a staking asset, concentrated institutional accumulation adds a new demand layer that is structurally different from retail or DeFi-native holders.

The Broader Corporate ETH Trend

BitMine is the most visible actor in what is becoming a broader pattern. Public companies and ETFs collectively hold over 10 million ETH as of mid-2026, with BlackRock's iShares Ethereum Trust alone holding 1.5 million ETH. As regulatory clarity around digital assets has improved in the United States following the CLARITY Act's legislative progress, the appetite among institutional treasurers to allocate to Ethereum has grown.

Whether the ETH treasury trend reaches the scale that the Bitcoin treasury movement has achieved — Strategy holds over 840,000 BTC, nearly 4% of Bitcoin's supply — remains to be seen. But BitMine's race toward 5% of Ethereum's supply is a data point that the ETH treasury model is developing its own momentum.
