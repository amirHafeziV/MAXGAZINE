About Quant
Trade Now →

Quant (QNT) is the native token of Quant Network, a technology company focused on solving one of blockchain's most persistent challenges: interoperability. Most blockchains operate in isolation — Ethereum cannot natively communicate with Hyperledger Fabric, and a Bitcoin payment cannot directly trigger a smart contract on another chain. Quant Network's flagship product, Overledger, is designed to bridge these silos through a software layer that sits above existing blockchains, connecting them without requiring any changes to their underlying code.

Overledger: The Blockchain Operating System

Overledger is sometimes described as an "operating system for blockchains." Rather than building a new blockchain or requiring each network to adopt a common standard, Overledger acts as a gateway or API layer that translates between different distributed ledger technologies. A developer can write a single application — called a multi-chain application, or mApp — that reads from and writes to multiple blockchains simultaneously, whether those are public networks like Ethereum and Bitcoin or private enterprise ledgers.

This architecture gives financial institutions and large enterprises a practical path to adopting blockchain without locking themselves into a single network. Banks can interact with multiple ledgers, regulators can monitor across chains, and interbank settlement or tokenized asset transfer can be orchestrated in a coordinated, auditable way.

QNT Token and Tokenomics

The QNT token plays a functional role in the Overledger ecosystem. Enterprises and developers that want to access Overledger services — including API calls, deploying mApps and interacting with connected ledgers — must hold or spend QNT. Licensing fees and service access are denominated in QNT, creating demand tied directly to real-world usage of the platform.

QNT has a fixed maximum supply of 14.6 million tokens. Unlike many crypto projects, this supply was set at launch and has not expanded, making QNT one of the smaller-supply tokens in the top-100 by market cap. The tight supply cap means that growth in Overledger usage tends to have a more concentrated effect on the token's economics.

Use Cases

Quant Network targets three main user groups: financial institutions, government bodies and enterprise technology companies. Central banks exploring Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDCs) have shown interest in Overledger as a neutral interoperability layer that can connect their digital currencies to existing payment rail infrastructure. Global banks testing tokenized deposit projects have used Overledger as the orchestration layer that allows different institutions' systems to communicate securely. Large enterprises seeking to connect their internal permissioned ledgers to public blockchains also represent a key market for the platform.

Quant Network was founded in 2015 by Gilbert Verdian, a cybersecurity veteran with a background advising governments and financial institutions on digital infrastructure. Verdian had worked with the UK National Health Service, the Treasury and the Bank of England, and through that experience he identified a critical gap: large institutions were beginning to explore blockchain, but no two organizations were likely to settle on the same platform. A layer that could connect those different systems without forcing everyone onto a single chain was, in his view, a necessary piece of infrastructure — one he decided to build.

The Overledger White Paper and Early Development

The concept of Overledger was formalized in a white paper published in 2018. The paper introduced the idea of a multi-chain operating system — software that could abstract blockchain differences the same way a conventional operating system abstracts hardware differences. Development accelerated through 2018, and in June of that year, Quant conducted a token generation event (TGE) that raised funds for further development and distributed QNT to early backers.

By the end of 2018, Overledger had released its first API, allowing developers to read from and write to multiple blockchains through a standardized interface. Partnerships with enterprise technology providers and integration of networks such as Ethereum, Bitcoin, XRP Ledger and Hyperledger Fabric helped demonstrate the platform's multi-chain credentials from an early stage.

Enterprise Adoption and Financial Sector Interest

Throughout 2019 and 2020, Quant Network secured a series of partnerships with banks, payment processors and technology integrators. The growing interest in central bank digital currencies added relevance to Quant's positioning: as central banks explored issuing digital versions of their currencies, the question of interoperability — how would a digital pound interact with a digital euro or a private stablecoin? — became a real policy problem, and Overledger positioned itself as an answer.

In 2020, Quant joined the SIA (Society for Interbank Automation) network, an Italian financial infrastructure firm, to explore blockchain interoperability for European banking. This signaled that Quant was not only targeting technology-forward crypto companies but also existing regulated financial infrastructure.

UK Finance and CBDC Involvement

By 2021 and 2022, Quant had become involved in formal explorations of programmable money and CBDCs in the United Kingdom. Gilbert Verdian participated in working groups and advisory bodies connected to the Bank of England's digital pound explorations. Quant Network also joined broader industry coalitions working on interoperability standards for tokenized assets and payments.

The UK Finance-led Great British Tokenized Deposit (GBTD) project emerged as one of the most high-profile initiatives where Overledger played a central role. This project, involving major banks including HSBC and Barclays, used Overledger as its interoperability layer to coordinate tokenized deposits across different institutions. It represented exactly the enterprise, multi-ledger use case that Quant had been designed for.

Market Cycles and Token Performance

QNT experienced significant price appreciation during the broader crypto bull market of 2020–2021, and later went through the market-wide corrections of 2022. Because QNT has a small maximum supply and its demand is intended to be driven by enterprise usage rather than speculation alone, its price behavior has at times diverged from broader market movements — a characteristic that attracted attention from investors looking for tokens with real utility-driven demand dynamics.

Quant Network's current story is shaped by two converging trends: the rise of tokenized real-world assets in traditional finance, and the acceleration of central bank digital currency pilots around the world. Both trends require the kind of cross-chain coordination that Overledger is designed to provide, and Quant has positioned itself at the intersection of blockchain innovation and institutional financial infrastructure.

Quant Flow and Programmable Money

In recent development cycles, Quant Network introduced Quant Flow, a money engine designed to make programmable money more accessible to banks and corporate treasury teams. Quant Flow allows organizations to set rules and conditions for how money moves — for example, triggering automated payments when certain conditions are met, or routing settlement funds across multiple ledgers. This is a natural extension of Overledger's multi-chain connectivity into the realm of smart finance operations.

The Great British Tokenized Deposit project, involving some of the UK's largest banks, marked a significant milestone in real-world validation for Overledger. This kind of regulated financial infrastructure deployment is what differentiates Quant from blockchain projects that primarily serve retail or DeFi audiences.

Regulatory Landscape and Institutional Tailwinds

As global regulators move toward clearer frameworks for tokenized assets, digital currencies and cross-border payments, Quant's enterprise focus is increasingly well-positioned. The company has maintained a strong presence in UK and European financial policy circles, and Gilbert Verdian remains active as a voice on blockchain interoperability in regulatory and standards-setting conversations.

Overledger now connects more than 45 networks, and the platform continues to expand its enterprise client base. The focus remains on the financial sector: banks, payment networks, custodians and governments looking to bridge their existing systems with blockchain-based infrastructure.

Market Outlook

Because QNT's utility is tied to enterprise contracts and platform usage rather than retail trading volume, its market dynamics differ from most crypto assets. Usage growth on Overledger — measured by API calls, new mApp deployments and enterprise contracts — feeds demand for the token in a more direct and contractual way than speculative token projects. With a fixed and relatively small supply, this utility-driven demand model remains central to QNT's long-term positioning in the market.
