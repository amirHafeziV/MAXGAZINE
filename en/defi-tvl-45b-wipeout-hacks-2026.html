DeFi Has Lost $45 Billion in 2026 — Hacks and Declining Prices Are Finishing What Each Other Started

DeFi's total value locked has fallen 39% year-to-date in 2026, from $115 billion to roughly $70 billion, as $942 million in hacks compound a broad crypto market correction — and only two of the top ten blockchains are still growing.

Published MAXGAZINE Desk · 2026-06-24
AD SPACEIn-article bannerBook this spot →

Decentralized finance entered 2026 under pressure and has not recovered. Total value locked (TVL) across all DeFi protocols has declined in every single month of the year, sliding from approximately $115 billion in January to roughly $70 billion as of June 24, 2026 — a 39% drop that erases gains made during the late-2025 bull run.

The decline is the product of two reinforcing forces: a broad crypto market correction that dragged asset prices lower, and a sustained wave of exploits that shook user confidence and accelerated withdrawals.

The Hack Wave

DeFi protocols have suffered approximately $942 million in exploit losses across 121 separate attacks so far in 2026, making Q2 the most-hacked quarter in the sector's history with 85 incidents. Two hacks stand out in scale.

In April 2026, KelpDAO — an Ethereum-based liquid restaking protocol — lost $290 million in what became the largest single DeFi exploit of the year. Blockchain analytics firm Chainalysis attributed the attack to North Korea's Lazarus Group, specifically the TraderTraitor sub-unit. The attackers did not exploit a smart contract flaw; instead they compromised internal RPC nodes and fed false data to a single-verifier bridge setup — a configuration that security researchers had flagged as dangerously centralized. In the days following the attack, Aave's TVL dropped from $26.4 billion to $14.3 billion as users across Ethereum-based protocols pulled funds.

The second largest incident of the year involved Drift Protocol, which lost approximately $285 million in a separate exploit with different methodology. Together, the two attacks account for nearly 60% of all DeFi losses in 2026.

The Chain Divergence

Ethereum, Solana, and Arbitrum — which together held the majority of DeFi TVL entering the year — have all posted sharp declines. Of the top ten blockchains by TVL, only TRON and Hyperliquid recorded net growth through June 2026.

TRON's resilience partly reflects its concentration in stablecoin settlement and USDT activity, which is less sensitive to price-driven TVL erosion. Hyperliquid's growth reflects continued user appetite for on-chain perpetual futures, one of the few DeFi sub-sectors that showed consistent volume growth even as underlying asset prices fell.

Institutional Skepticism

A JPMorgan research note in June 2026 observed that recurring exploits and stagnant TVL are "continuing to limit institutional appeal" for DeFi. The report noted that the absence of credible on-chain insurance, the complexity of smart contract auditing, and the bridge infrastructure risk exposed by the KelpDAO attack remain unresolved barriers to meaningful institutional capital allocation.

What Comes Next

Protocol teams across the ecosystem have responded by accelerating security upgrades. Multi-DVN bridge configurations — which would have prevented the KelpDAO attack — are now being mandated by several major protocols. On-chain insurance primitives from providers such as Nexus Mutual and Neptune Finance have seen increased coverage demand in the months since the KelpDAO hack.

Whether that is enough to restore confidence remains uncertain. With Bitcoin down roughly 20% from its May 2026 peak and risk-off sentiment building across global markets as of late June, DeFi's TVL recovery depends on both protocol security improvements and a broader macro tailwind that has not yet materialized.
