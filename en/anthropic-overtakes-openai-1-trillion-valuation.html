Anthropic Overtakes OpenAI in Private Valuation, Nears $1 Trillion

Anthropic's $965 billion valuation following its Series H marks a historic milestone — the first time a company other than OpenAI has led the private AI leaderboard.

Published 2026-06-14 · MAXGAZINE Desk

A Historic Reshuffling at the Top of AI

Anthropic has overtaken OpenAI in private company valuation, closing a $65 billion Series H round at a valuation of approximately $965 billion, according to reports from multiple sources tracking AI funding rounds in June 2026. The development marks the first time since OpenAI's meteoric rise that another company has topped the private AI leaderboard — a seismic shift in the landscape of artificial intelligence.

The company's ascent has been fuelled by extraordinary revenue growth. From $14 billion in annualized recurring revenue at the close of its previous Series G funding round in February, Anthropic grew its ARR to a $47 billion run-rate by late May 2026 — a more than three-fold increase in under four months. This pace of revenue growth is nearly unprecedented for a company at this scale.

What Drives Anthropic's Growth

Anthropic's flagship Claude model family has gained significant enterprise traction. The company's focus on safety, reliability, and interpretability has resonated strongly with large enterprise customers in regulated industries including finance, healthcare, and legal services — sectors where OpenAI's more permissive deployment approach has sometimes raised compliance concerns.

The broader competitive landscape is shifting rapidly. In the Anthropic vs. OpenAI contest that is shaping up as the defining rivalry of the AI era, both companies are expanding aggressively into developer tools, enterprise APIs, and agentic AI platforms. Claude's growing adoption in coding assistants, customer service automation, and research applications has been central to the revenue surge.

Industry Implications

The valuation milestone arrives as the AI investment cycle shows no signs of decelerating. The 50 most-funded AI startups in June 2026 span foundation models, robotics, agentic platforms, and vertical applications. Humanoid robotics in particular has emerged as a breakout category, with companies like SkildAI attracting record capital. The race is no longer just about model benchmarks — it is about who can build the most durable enterprise workflows.
