Kraken in Talks to Buy 15% of Aave at $385M as DeFi Lending Stages a Quiet Comeback

Crypto exchange Kraken is weighing a $71 million investment in Aave — the largest decentralized lending protocol — as a first move under a new strategic investment unit ahead of Kraken's own IPO. Meanwhile, Aave V4 just crossed $200 million in deposits, suggesting DeFi lending may be bottoming.

Crypto exchange Kraken, operating under parent company Payward Inc., is in advanced discussions to acquire a 15% stake in Aave Group — the entity behind the largest decentralized lending protocol — at a valuation of $385 million, according to reporting from CoinDesk published June 25. The proposed deal would see Kraken invest 35,000 ETH in exchange for 250,000 AAVE tokens and a 15% common equity stake, with a total deal value of approximately $71 million.

The investment is framed as the first in a series of strategic deals under Payward Asset Management, a newly created unit that Kraken is using to diversify its business before a planned initial public offering. Kraken is reportedly looking to syndicate part of the investment to other buyers, suggesting it is positioning the deal as much as a strategic signal as a financial commitment.

Why Aave, and Why Now

Aave is the dominant decentralized lending protocol, allowing users to deposit crypto assets into liquidity pools to earn yield, while borrowers post collateral against which they take out overcollateralized loans. At its peak, the protocol held over $20 billion in total value locked. That figure collapsed alongside the broader DeFi market in 2022 and again in 2026, when a series of exploits and the ongoing bear market eroded confidence in decentralized finance broadly.

The timing of Kraken's interest coincides with a genuine recovery signal. Aave V4 — the protocol's most significant architectural upgrade to date, introducing a hub-and-spoke design that reduces liquidity fragmentation across chains — crossed $200 million in deposits by June 24, less than three months after launch. That milestone came after the V4 deposit base doubled in a single month, suggesting the new architecture is attracting fresh capital from institutional players.

The proposed deal would also come with governance rights, giving Kraken a seat at the table for a DeFi protocol whose fee revenues and treasury are likely to grow if the lending recovery continues. For Aave Group's founders, the deal offers something equally valuable: the institutional credibility and exchange distribution of one of crypto's largest and most regulated centralized venues.

The Bigger Context: CeFi Reaching Into DeFi

Kraken's Aave move is part of a broader trend of centralized exchanges and financial institutions taking direct stakes in DeFi protocols as the lines between the two worlds blur. Earlier in 2026, several banks and asset managers moved tokenized funds on-chain, and stablecoin settlement volumes on DeFi infrastructure began outpacing traditional correspondent banking routes for certain corridors.

Whether the deal closes — and at what final terms — has not been confirmed. A representative for Kraken declined to comment to press outlets. Aave's AAVE token surged approximately 15% in the 24 hours following the report, a reaction that suggests markets view the potential partnership as a meaningful validation of the protocol's fundamentals.
