About

Gate Token (GT) is the native utility token of Gate.io, a centralized cryptocurrency exchange that has operated since 2013. GT serves multiple functions within the Gate.io ecosystem, most notably granting holders discounts on trading fees, access to exclusive token sales on the platform's Startup launchpad, and higher tiers in Gate.io's VIP program, which unlocks additional fee reductions and services.

Holders can also stake GT to earn additional rewards and participate in various platform promotions. Gate.io regularly conducts GT buyback-and-burn operations using a share of its trading revenues, removing tokens from circulation to create deflationary pressure on the supply over time. This mechanism is similar to exchange-token models employed by other major trading platforms.

GT operates on the GateChain network, Gate.io's own public blockchain, which is designed to provide on-chain storage of digital assets with enhanced security features including a vault account system that allows users to recover funds in the event of a private key compromise. GateChain uses a Proof-of-Stake consensus model and supports smart contracts, enabling developers to build decentralized applications on its network.

Beyond its exchange-centric use cases, GT functions as the native token for GateChain transactions and DeFi applications built on that network. This dual role — combining the demand generated by an active trading platform with the utility of a programmable blockchain — positions GT as both an exchange token and a general-purpose network asset.

History

Gate.io was founded in 2013 by Lin Han under the name Bter before rebranding and expanding its offering. It is one of the earliest cryptocurrency exchanges to remain active through successive market cycles, providing spot trading, futures, options, and other financial products across hundreds of digital assets. The exchange's long operating history gave it a broad international user base, particularly in Asia and among retail traders seeking access to a wide selection of altcoins.

Gate Token (GT) was introduced as Gate.io's platform token to reward active users, with the token distribution including a portion allocated to the exchange's users through trading-fee rebates and loyalty programs. From its early days, GT functioned primarily as a fee-discount token, with additional utility added as the exchange grew and diversified its product suite.

GateChain, the blockchain designed to support GT and extend its functionality, was launched in 2020. The project introduced the vault account concept, which differentiates GateChain's security model from standard blockchain wallets by allowing users to set a revocable key that can retrieve funds from a vault account within a set delay period before a transaction finalizes — a safeguard against theft or unauthorized access.

Through 2021 and 2022, GT benefited from the broader growth of the exchange during the bull market, as Gate.io listed many emerging tokens early and attracted traders seeking assets not yet available on larger platforms. The regular buyback-and-burn mechanism introduced during this period became a key part of GT's tokenomics narrative, linking the token's value directly to the exchange's trading volume and profitability.

By the mid-2020s, Gate.io had established itself as one of the top global cryptocurrency exchanges by volume, with GT trading as one of the more liquid exchange tokens in the market alongside those issued by Binance and OKX.

Recent Developments

Gate.io continues to operate as one of the largest global exchanges by trading volume and listed assets, offering users access to thousands of trading pairs across spot, derivatives and structured products. GT remains the central token binding together this ecosystem, and its value is closely tied to the ongoing health of exchange activity — higher volumes translate directly into more fees available for the buyback-and-burn program.

GateChain and its DeFi ecosystem have remained active, though the broader competition among Layer-1 blockchains means GateChain occupies a relatively niche position compared to networks like Ethereum and BNB Chain. Gate.io has compensated for this by integrating its platform wallet and financial services tightly with the GateChain infrastructure, creating a more cohesive product for users who hold and use GT within the exchange's own services.

The exchange has expanded into Web3 products, NFT marketplaces, copy trading and a range of yield-bearing products, with GT often integrated as the preferred currency for fees or access tiers across these features. For investors, GT represents a bet on Gate.io's continued relevance and growth — an exchange that has survived multiple bear markets and regulatory shifts, serving a global user base that spans both retail and institutional segments.
